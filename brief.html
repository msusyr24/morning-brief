Mental Model · Economics/Business

Principle-Agent Problem

When one party (agent) acts on behalf of another (principal), their incentives rarely perfectly align. Lawyers bill by the hour, not by outcome. CEOs optimize for short-term stock price, not long-term company health. The discipline: in any relationship, ask whose incentives actually match yours.

🧬 Health & Science

Is the Correlation Between Hypertrophy and Strength Gains Stronger Than We Realized?

Stronger by Science revisits the relationship between muscle growth and strength gains, arguing that prior studies likely underestimated hypertrophy's contribution to strength in novice lifters — partly due to measurement error in muscle size estimates that attenuated the observed correlation. The corrected picture suggests hypertrophy matters more for new lifters than previously thought, but the relationship is still far from deterministic: you can gain muscle without gaining proportional strength, and vice versa. Worth reading if you program for both hypertrophy and strength outcomes.

Stronger by Science

Lung cancer screening in never-smokers: the data

Peter Attia makes the case that low-dose CT lung screening is evidence-supported even for people who have never smoked, despite current guidelines not recommending it for this population. The argument is that lung cancer kills a meaningful number of never-smokers, early detection dramatically improves survival odds, and the risk-benefit math is favorable enough to make it a rational personal decision independent of guideline status — a classic Attia "the guidelines are behind the evidence" framing.

Peter Attia MD

GLP-1 drugs and marriage

A new study using the Understanding America Study finds that single women starting GLP-1s for weight loss see a 29% increase in marriage/cohabitation rates compared to matched controls who wanted to start GLP-1s but hadn't yet. This is a striking downstream social effect of weight loss drugs beyond metabolic outcomes — and raises interesting questions about the compound effects of GLP-1s on confidence, energy, and social behavior.
