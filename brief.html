Mental Model · Economics/Business

Principle-Agent Problem

When one party (agent) acts on behalf of another (principal), their incentives rarely perfectly align. Lawyers bill by the hour, not by outcome. CEOs optimize for short-term stock price, not long-term company health. The discipline: in any relationship, ask whose incentives actually match yours.

🎥 From People I Follow

Worth your time:

Jared Alderman

A genuinely interesting conversation about where poker edge actually comes from in 2025. The core argument: GTO knowledge has become so commoditized that the real frontier is psychological self-awareness and live-tell reading — not more solver work. Alderman and guest Charlie make the case that most elite players still have glaring emotional blind spots (a bad text that morning, a cooler two hands ago) that cost more EV than any theoretical leak, and that the poker world is about 5 years from the high-stakes community systematically studying live tells and 15 years from embracing the psychological/emotional layer. The specific live-tells framework Charlie describes is teachable and systematic — not "vibes," but pattern-matching specific behaviors to specific time windows in a hand — and he argues it makes the game feel "unfair" once you have it. The meta-point: being one level above your opponent in self-knowledge is the ripest edge available right now, and almost nobody is working on it.

Videos: Poker's Next Frontier Is Not What You Think

Peter Attia

Attia and Layne Norton go deep on whether industrial seed oil processing — not linoleic acid itself — is the real problem. Norton walks through the hexane extraction process in detail: the solvent has a low boiling point (69°C), residual amounts in finished oils are typically 0.05–0.5 parts per million, hexane toxicity is primarily via inhalation not ingestion, and the rodent-study doses required to cause mild liver/neurotoxicity translate to consuming ~11,340 kg of oil. Norton's position: hexane is almost certainly a non-issue given those numbers and the fact that it doesn't bioaccumulate. The more interesting unresolved question — which Attia keeps pushing on — is whether lifetime chronic low-dose exposure to oxidized lipid byproducts (from reheating, not extraction) represents a different risk category that pure linoleic acid studies wouldn't capture.

Let Other People Witness Your Actions — Commentary on Epictetus Handbook §35

Donald Robertson's close reading of Epictetus on the relationship between action and audience: the Stoic position isn't that you should perform virtue for others, but that your actions should be the kind you'd be comfortable having witnessed — a useful internal test. Short and worth sitting with if you're working through the Handbook systematically.
