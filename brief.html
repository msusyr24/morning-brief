Mental Model · Economics/Business

Principle-Agent Problem

When one party (agent) acts on behalf of another (principal), their incentives rarely perfectly align. Lawyers bill by the hour, not by outcome. CEOs optimize for short-term stock price, not long-term company health. The discipline: in any relationship, ask whose incentives actually match yours.

Peter Attia (with Layne Norton)

The seed oil debate gets a more rigorous treatment than usual here. Norton's central argument: the hexane extraction concern is mostly a red herring — residuals in finished oil are under 0.5 parts per million, hexane toxicity is primarily from inhalation not ingestion, and you'd need to consume ~11,000 kg of oil to approach mild side effects. More interesting is his framing on oxidation: meaningful oxidation of seed oils requires temperatures well above 200°C sustained for hours, which doesn't happen during processing — the real oxidation risk is in repeated high-heat cooking by the consumer, not manufacturing. The linoleic acid displacement argument (from ~3% of food availability 100 years ago to ~10% today) is flagged as the more credible concern, not industrial processing per se.

Renaissance Periodization (Dr. Mike)

A brief but concrete intra-workout nutrition protocol: training fasted, Dr. Mike uses a single large container with 45g whey protein, 30g carbs from Gatorade, a full LMNT electrolyte packet, and "multiple liters" of water — mixed together and consumed across the session plus the ride home, before eating a post-workout meal. 🧬 Health & Science

Acetaminophen vs. Ibuprofen — The Mystery in the Medicine Cabinet

A long-form piece in Asterisk Magazine examining what we actually know (and don't) about two of the most-used drugs on earth. The core puzzle: acetaminophen's mechanism of action remains poorly understood despite 70+ years of use, and comparative efficacy trials show ibuprofen is meaningfully superior for most pain types — yet acetaminophen remains the default recommendation, particularly during pregnancy and for people with GI issues. Worth reading for anyone who reflexively reaches for Tylenol.

Asterisk Magazine

Peter Attia: Prostate Cancer Screening — Why PSA Guidelines Are Failing Men

Attia's guest argues that current PSA screening guidelines (which broadly discourage routine screening) are causing men to present with late-stage, incurable prostate cancer rather than early-stage, treatable disease. The key claim: "the timing of when you find the cancer is not a minor detail — it's arguably the determining factor in life and death." The episode covers modern tools beyond basic PSA (MRI-guided biopsy, PSA density, genetic risk stratification) that can dramatically reduce false positives while catching more meaningful cancers early.

Peter Attia MD — Episode #388

Contact Lens That Monitors and Treats Glaucoma via Microfluidics

Researchers have built a smart contact lens that uses microfluidic channels to continuously measure intraocular pressure and, when pressure spikes, release a precise dose of glaucoma medication directly to the eye — no drops, no patient compliance required. This is a meaningful convergence of biosensing and drug delivery in a form factor that removes the primary failure mode of glaucoma treatment (patients forgetting or skipping eye drops).

💡 Worth Reading

The Brand Age — Paul Graham

Graham argues we're entering an era where brand — meaning the accumulated trust and reputation of a name — is becoming more valuable, not less, precisely because AI makes the underlying product increasingly commoditized and easy to replicate. The scarcest thing becomes the signal of who to trust. Worth sitting with as a mental model for how value accrues in a world where execution gets cheaper.

Paul Graham

Five Crucial Stoic Ideas — Massimo Pigliucci

Pigliucci examines five core Stoic doctrines — the dichotomy of control, living according to nature, the preferred indifferents, the discipline of desire, and the practice of memento mori — and stress-tests each against modern philosophy and empirical science. Rather than pure hagiography, he notes where Stoicism's ancient metaphysics doesn't hold up and where the practical ethics remains robust. Useful for anyone who uses Stoicism as a live practice rather than just inspiration.

What Marcus Aurelius Can Teach Us About Coping with Stress — Donald Robertson

Robertson's conversation with Mark Forstater covers the practical stress-management toolkit embedded in the Meditations — specifically how Marcus used negative visualization, the view from above, and the discipline of assent (pausing between stimulus and response) during the unprecedented pressures of plague, war, and imperial governance. The framing as a coping manual rather than a philosophical text is the right one for practical use.

Diverse Organic Molecules Found on Mars

The Mars Curiosity rover's first use of a TMAH (tetramethylammonium hydroxide) thermochemolysis experiment revealed a significantly more complex array of organic molecules than previously detected — including aromatic and aliphatic compounds preserved for potentially billions of years. This doesn't confirm life, but it substantially expands the known chemical complexity of the Martian surface and the potential building blocks that were present.
