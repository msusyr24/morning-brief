🧬 Health & Science

Is the Correlation Between Hypertrophy and Strength Gains Stronger Than We Realized?

Stronger By Science digs into whether muscle growth causes strength gains or just correlates with them — a question with direct programming implications. Their conclusion: hypertrophy probably contributes more to strength in novice lifters than prior studies suggested, but the relationship is nowhere near as tight as surface-level correlations imply. Practically, this means training for size and training for strength overlap substantially early on but diverge as you advance — the neuromuscular and skill components of strength become increasingly independent of muscle mass.

Stronger by Science

Environmental Pollution and Longevity: Air, Noise, Light, EMFs

Attia's AMA #87 covers the 80/20 framework for reducing environmental pollution load without lifestyle paralysis — specifically air pollution (the highest-impact modifiable factor), noise pollution (linked to cardiovascular risk via cortisol/sleep disruption), light exposure at night, and EMFs. The framing is explicitly about practical mitigation rather than comprehensive elimination, which is the right lens for this material.

The Economic Effects of GLP-1s

A new study using Danish administrative data and a difference-in-differences design measures what happens to labor market outcomes when patients start semaglutide. This is one of the first causal (not just correlational) estimates of GLP-1s' effect on work, income, and productivity — the kind of real-world economic data that will eventually shape insurance coverage and public health policy far more than clinical trials alone.
