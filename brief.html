Thomas DeLauer

DeLauer covers a Nature Metabolism study that tracked 3,000+ proteins across a 7-day fast. Key finding: after 36-48 hours, muscle protein breakdown markers actually declined, not increased — nitrogen excretion was minimal even at day 7, contradicting the common "fasting is catabolic" concern. More interesting: the extracellular matrix (ECM) showed significant collagen turnover changes after 48 hours, driven partly by FGF-21, suggesting fasting may actively repair connective tissue rather than degrade it. The study also tracked days 8-14 post-fast and found that eating inflammatory foods (seed oils, refined carbs) immediately after a long fast blunted the immune reset and pro-inflammatory cytokine reductions — meaning the 2-3 days after breaking a multi-day fast matter as much as the fast itself. In a separate video, he covers a 32-person RCT showing that blueberry bioactives improved insulin sensitivity by 22.2% vs. 4.9% placebo over 6 weeks — with no change in body weight, calories, or activity — attributing the mechanism to anthocyanins improving endothelial nitric oxide (better insulin delivery to muscle) and AMPK activation (better glucose uptake at the cell), with a quantum biology tangent on electron tunneling efficiency in the mitochondrial electron transport chain.
