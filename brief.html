Mental Model · Risk/Mathematics

Convexity vs. Concavity

Convex payoffs benefit from volatility (options, bets with capped losses). Concave payoffs are hurt by volatility (leverage, short-term trading). Life structures that are convex compound in your favor; concave structures blow up eventually. Structure your life to be convex where possible.

Peter Attia

A comprehensive AMA on cardiorespiratory fitness as the single strongest modifiable predictor of all-cause mortality — outperforming LDL, blood pressure, BMI, even age in the data. The core claim: going from bottom quartile to top quartile VO₂ max cuts mortality risk by ~50%; top 2–3% vs bottom 20% is a 4–5x difference. His practical framework uses the triangle metaphor (wide base = sustained aerobic capacity, high peak = VO₂ max), built through zone 2 (base) and zone 5 intervals (peak). Separate expert interview with Abbie Smith-Ryan on women's training specifics: for a time-constrained woman (~3 hrs/week), she recommends 2×30-min full-body resistance sessions (6–8 reps, 60–80% 1RM, 30s between exercises) plus 2 HIIT sessions (10×1 min on/off at near-max effort) — prioritizing resistance over pure cardio. Wearable VO₂ max estimates (Apple Watch, Garmin, etc.) are essentially useless — they can be off by 20 points because they can't measure gas exchange and use flawed optical heart rate sensors.

🧬 Health & Science

The ApoC3 Longevity Marker Most Doctors Don't Test

Nick Norwitz's deep dive into ApoC3 makes a compelling case that modern cardiology is "focusing the spotlight in the wrong place." In studies of centenarians, the ApoC3 loss-of-function variant is 2.5x more prevalent than in controls — and the cardiovascular benefit is explicitly independent of LDL/ApoB (LDL drops ~4%; remnant cholesterol drops ~43%). ApoC3 works by suppressing lipoprotein lipase, slowing fat clearance; it's upregulated by glucose and especially fructose. Practical interventions: low-carb/ketogenic diets reduce ApoC3 by ~50% within 2 weeks; omega-3s also reduce it; excess fructose is the strongest driver upward. You can ask your doctor for an ApoC3 blood test — it's rarely ordered but reveals a dimension of cardiovascular risk that standard lipid panels miss entirely.

Nick Norwitz video

Gut Bacteria → Creatine → Brain Energy → Depression: An RCT

A Cell Metabolism paper found that stress can alter the microbiome to reduce creatine absorption — leading to elevated creatine in stool, reduced creatine in blood and cerebrospinal fluid, and measurable brain energy deficits linked to depression. Supplementing with specific Bifidobacterium strains + creatine restored uptake. In a 45-person placebo-controlled trial, the combination produced significant improvement in depression scores. The actionable implication: if gut health is impaired (possibly from ultra-processed food), creatine supplementation may matter beyond muscle function — it may support brain energy metabolism directly. This complements existing evidence that exercise-induced autophagy and mitochondrial health improve mood.

Shanna Swan on Endocrine Disruptors: The Taint-to-Testosterone Connection

Dr. Swan appeared on Peter Attia's podcast with a practical kit to test your own phthalate/bisphenol/paraben load via urine. Key data points: microplastics and plasticizers correlate with reduced anogenital distance (taint size) in male infants — a validated developmental biomarker — and with declining testosterone, reduced fertility, and increased miscarriage rates, independent of body weight. The most actionable single change: stop drinking hot liquids from paper cups or cans (which have plastic linings), switch to French press coffee, and store food in glass or food-grade silicone rather than plastic. She also flagged that most cooking water filters do remove these compounds, though distillation is the most thorough method.
