Nick Norwitz

Norwitz argues that the "lower LDL is always better" paradigm is incomplete for longevity. He presents data showing PCSK9 loss-of-function mutations dramatically lower LDL but show no lifespan benefit—if anything, trending toward earlier death. The key variable he identifies is ApoC3, a protein that slows triglyceride clearance. Centenarians were 2.5x more likely to carry ApoC3 loss-of-function variants, yet their LDL/ApoB levels were virtually identical to controls—the difference was higher HDL and lower triglyceride-to-HDL ratio. Actionable strategies to lower ApoC3: ketogenic diets (one study showed 50% reduction in 2 weeks), fructose restriction (worse than glucose for ApoC3), and omega-3s. He also covered HRV research: low HRV associates with 50% higher heart attack risk in healthy people and 5.3x higher mortality post-heart attack.
