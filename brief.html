🧬 Health & Science

Is the Correlation Between Hypertrophy and Strength Gains Stronger Than We Realized?

Stronger by Science digs into whether muscle growth actually drives strength gains — a question with real programming implications. The finding: hypertrophy probably contributes more to strength development in newer lifters than prior studies suggested, but the relationship is nowhere near as tight as it first appears. The practical takeaway is nuanced — size and strength training are related but not interchangeable, and assuming hypertrophy automatically translates to strength (or vice versa) remains an oversimplification.

For the First Time, a Cell Built from Scratch Grows and Divides

Researchers have constructed a synthetic cell from non-living components that can grow and divide — a milestone that has been a central goal of synthetic biology for decades. This isn't a tweaked organism; it's a cell assembled from scratch that replicates the core behavior of life. The implications span origins-of-life research, synthetic biology applications, and our fundamental understanding of what "life" requires at a minimum.

Donald Robertson, the leading Marcus Aurelius biographer, pushes back on the popular caricature of Stoics as cold and unfeeling. This corrects a widespread misreading that causes people to mistake emotional suppression for Stoic discipline.
