Mental Model · Psychology

Deprival Super-Reaction

Losing $100 feels worse than finding $100 feels good — by about 2x, per Kahneman's research. Munger calls this 'deprival super-reaction.' It explains stock-market panics, breakup grief, and why people fight harder to keep what they have than to acquire what they don't. Knowing this, you can price losses more rationally.

🧬 Health & Science

Apigenin: Top Compound in 66-Natural-Compound Longevity Screen

A 2025 paper in Advanced Science screened 66 natural compounds for anti-aging activity using human cell cultures and found apigenin (abundant in parsley) as the standout — not by killing senescent cells, but by suppressing SASP (senescence-associated secretory phenotype), the inflammatory signal cascade those cells broadcast (specifically IL-6, IL-8, CXCL10). In irradiated mice with induced premature aging, apigenin reversed fur graying, restored muscle strength, and improved lung and spleen tissue without eliminating the senescent cells themselves (which still perform useful functions). A complementary mechanism: apigenin inhibits CD38, the enzyme that degrades NAD with age, meaning it addresses both the inflammatory-noise problem and the NAD-depletion problem simultaneously. Practical dose signal: ~30mg from 2 tbsp fresh flat-leaf parsley with a fat-containing meal (improves absorption), or 50-100mg supplement. Pair with spermidine in a fasted window for mitophagy benefit.

Via Thomas DeLauer

Extended Fasting (36hr) vs. 16:8 for Insulin Resistance and Visceral Fat

Clinical argument from a practicing physician: intermittent fasting windows under 24 hours fail to deplete glycogen sufficiently to normalize insulin, which means lipolysis from visceral fat (the hormonally active, metabolically damaging fat depot) is never properly unlocked. The mechanism is specific — insulin inactivates hormone-sensitive lipase (HSL), the enzyme that liberates stored fat from fat cells, meaning you can be in a calorie deficit and still be in "storage mode." His 6-week clinical protocol (36-hour fast, once weekly) reportedly produces 50% reduction in body fat mass and 60% reduction in visceral fat with negligible muscle loss (~2%), because the target isn't calorie deficit per se but normalization of the insulin-glucagon axis. This is meaningfully different from conventional calorie restriction, which triggers metabolic adaptation without addressing the hormonal environment. The acute-vs-chronic stress distinction also matters: acute fasting stress is adaptive; chronic mild restriction is not.

Via Thomas DeLauer

AMPK Activation Without Exercise: ATX-304 in Phase 2 Trials

ATX-304 is a compound that activates AMPK — the intracellular "fuel gauge" that exercise normally flips — driving glucose into muscles without requiring insulin. In Type 1 diabetic animals (no insulin production whatsoever), it normalized blood glucose, protected against obesity, and improved endurance. It's currently in Phase 2 human trials and reportedly being used recreationally. This is relevant to anyone interested in metabolic health and insulin resistance: it suggests AMPK-pathway activation is a viable independent lever, separate from both insulin and GLP-1 mechanisms.

Via Nick Norwitz

Insulin Resistance Precedes Blood Glucose Elevation by ~20 Years

Dr. Ekberg's breakdown of 10 pre-diagnostic signs of insulin resistance is worth having on your radar: the HOMA-IR formula (glucose × insulin ÷ 405) can reveal someone is 3.4x more insulin resistant than baseline while their glucose reads nearly identical (90 vs. 92 mg/dL). Fasting insulin is the sensitive early marker; fasting glucose is a controlled variable the body defends for decades. Early warning signs include high triglycerides + low HDL (the classic dyslipidemia pattern), creeping blood pressure (insulin causes renal sodium retention), elevated uric acid (impaired kidney excretion), and post-meal hunger 1-2 hours later (insulin overshoot causing rapid drop, triggering hunger response). Clinically underutilized: uric acid as an early-warning marker that often shifts before lipids or glucose.

Via Dr. Sten Ekberg

Hypertrophy–Strength Correlation Stronger in New Lifters Than Previously Estimated

Stronger by Science's new analysis revisits the relationship between muscle growth and strength gains, finding the correlation is probably larger in novice lifters than prior studies indicated — muscle growth contributes more to early strength development than the field had assumed. However, the headline finding is tempered: the relationship is nowhere near as strong as it appears on the surface once statistical confounds are accounted for, meaning muscle size and strength remain meaningfully separate training outcomes even for beginners.

Stoicism Is Not Unemotional

Donald Robertson (Marcus Aurelius biographer) pushes back on the popular misconception that Stoicism requires emotional suppression or being "like a man made of oak or stone." The Stoics distinguished between unhealthy passions (driven by false judgments) and healthy emotions (eupatheiai) — the Stoic sage still experiences joy, caution, and wishing, just not fear, lust, or distress. This matters practically: the goal isn't emotional flatness, it's emotional accuracy. Useful framing for anyone applying Stoicism to real adversity rather than performing detachment.
