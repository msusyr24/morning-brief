Jared Alderman

Alderman zeroes in on the gap between insight and execution — the moment a coaching breakthrough feels profound but produces zero behavioral change. His core argument: "aha moments" create a kind of mental applause that masquerades as progress while nothing actually changes. The fix isn't willpower; it's systematizing the insight into a concrete, repeatable technique you can pull from your toolkit under pressure, specifically when you're a "weaker mental version of yourself." He draws on a Buddhist framing — everything is causes and conditions, including mental states — to argue that concentration, calm, and clarity aren't virtues you summon, they're outputs of conditions you can learn to engineer. His new "Poker Performance Field Kit" is designed as guided in-session audio for specific moments (just lost a big pot, entering day two of a tournament), meant to bridge theory to live execution.

GTO Wizard

GTO Wizard launched PLO (Pot-Limit Omaha) solver coverage, and this stream with Tombos21 and Joey Ingram surfaced several sharp insights for Texas Hold'em players crossing over. Key findings from the solver: (1) When facing a four-bet in PLO, the four-bettor has aces roughly 86% of the time, making hands like KK a near-pure fold — a complete inversion of NLHE intuition. (2) Calling dominates three-betting preflop across most positions because equities run much closer in PLO (four cards means even "ragged" hands can connect hard), making pot inflation OOP unattractive. (3) Tombos' central mindset shift: in PLO, your hand strength right now is nearly irrelevant — what matters is your hand's potential by the river, including backdoor draws and future blocker utility. The leading range on boards that hit your opponent hard (e.g., J-7-5) should be bigger, not a small range-bet — an immediate lesson from the solver that contradicts common NLHE heuristics about check-to-the-aggressor.

GTO Lab

Clemen Deng makes a tight, specific point: on J-7-5 boards where the blinds have strong range coverage (7x and 5x combos), range-betting small is inefficient because it doesn't exploit your range advantage — instead, the correct play is a larger sizing or checking ~40% of the time. When the board pairs (a 7 comes on the turn) and your opponent checks, they're weighted heavily toward unpaired hands, at which point you should start leveraging your ace-high advantage and bet near-pure with the right holdings. The takeaway: board texture should dictate whether you're attacking or conceding range advantage, not defaulting to small range-bets.

Peter Attia + Layne Norton: Seed Oil Processing — Is Industrial Extraction the Real Problem?

The conversation focused specifically on hexane extraction of seed oils. Norton's argument: the hexane residue in finished oils is 0.05–0.5 parts per million (often below instrument detection limits), hexane toxicity is primarily via inhalation not ingestion, rodent lethality studies required 5,000 mg/kg bodyweight, and the human equivalent dose to experience mild side effects would require consuming ~11,340 kg of oil. His broader point is that chronic bioaccumulation is implausible given hexane doesn't bioaccumulate and the body clears it. The more open question they leave unresolved: linoleic acid's share of total food availability has grown from <3% to ~10% over the past century — whether that dietary shift matters more than the processing method is still contested.
