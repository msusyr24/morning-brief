🧬 Health & Science

Prostate Cancer: A PSA on PSA

Peter Attia argues that PSA screening is underutilized despite being one of the most effective early-detection tools available for prostate cancer — a disease where early detection dramatically changes outcomes. His core frustration: guidelines have become overly cautious in response to overtreatment concerns, leading physicians to under-screen men who would actually benefit. The piece is a case for proactive, personalized screening rather than waiting for population-level guidelines to catch up.

💡 Worth Reading

Why Japan Has Such Good Railways

A deep structural analysis of Japan's rail system — not a tourist piece, but an examination of the institutional, regulatory, and urban-planning decisions that made it work. The core argument is that Japan's railways succeeded because operators were given land development rights alongside rail rights, aligning incentives in ways Western systems never did. Genuinely useful as a mental model for how infrastructure and incentive design interact.

The Electromechanical Angle Computer Inside the B-52's Star Tracker

A detailed teardown of the analog computer that the B-52 bomber used to navigate by stars — before GPS, before digital computers were flight-ready. The engineering elegance here is striking: a purely mechanical system solving spherical trigonometry in real time using gears, cams, and resolvers. A beautiful example of first-principles engineering under severe constraints, and a good reminder of how much human ingenuity predates the silicon era.
