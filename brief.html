🎥 From People I Follow

Worth your time:

Nick Norwitz

Solid explainer on Heart Rate Variability (HRV) as a predictor of mortality — a 1987 study of 808 post-heart-attack patients found low HRV conferred a 5.3x higher risk of death over subsequent years, and a separate prospective study found even a one standard deviation drop in HRV associated with 47% higher risk of a first cardiac event. Norwitz explains the physiology clearly: HRV reflects parasympathetic (vagal) tone — high variability means a responsive, adaptive nervous system; low variability means you're "flying down the highway with broken brakes." For improving HRV: Zone 2 cardio is the most reliable baseline intervention; HIIT can drive meaningful gains when dosed correctly (he does 20 min, 1-2x/week) but suppresses HRV acutely if overdone; omega-3s (3,000–4,000mg EPA+DHA/day, equivalent to ~3 cans of sardines) are shown in RCTs to boost HRV via membrane fluidity, reduced inflammation, and direct vagal tone effects. His personal HRV peak coincided with a sardine-heavy dietary phase delivering 6,000mg/day of EPA+DHA.

GTO Wizard

Tom "Boss21" walks Joey Ingram through the unified math behind pot odds, MDF, and bluffing frequency — making the case that these aren't separate concepts but two sides of the same coin. The key insight demonstrated via GTO Wizard node-locking: on the river, a solver betting 150% pot needs to be bluffing exactly 37% of the time to make bluff-catchers indifferent; shift the bluff frequency just slightly in either direction (even by 3-4 combos) and the correct response snaps entirely to always-call or always-fold. This extreme sensitivity to small frequency deviations is why river play is the most punishing street for population leaks, and why detecting those leaks in real-time is extremely hard — even skilled players can't distinguish 35% vs. 41% bluff frequency from small samples.

🧬 Health & Science

Sun Exposure: The Actual Evidence (Rowan Jacobsen on JRE)

Several specific, data-grounded claims worth filing away. Outdoor workers have lower melanoma incidence than office workers — melanoma is associated with intermittent high-dose burning (especially in childhood), not chronic moderate exposure. UVA penetrates window glass while UVB does not, meaning you can accumulate carcinogenic UV exposure sitting by a window or driving without the tanning response that signals overexposure — which may partly explain "truck driver face" (unilateral photoaging from driver-side window UVA). Large clinical trials on vitamin D supplementation (including a major NEJM-published analysis) failed to show benefits for cancer, cardiovascular, or immune outcomes in people who weren't severely deficient — whereas people with naturally high serum vitamin D (from sun) consistently show lower rates of chronic disease, suggesting sunlight produces companion compounds or signaling molecules the pill doesn't replicate. Sunlight triggers endogenous opioid release, lowers blood pressure via nitric oxide-mediated vasodilation, and improves cognition and metabolic rate — all independent of vitamin D. The dermatology establishment's zero-sun recommendation is tailored to fair-skinned melanoma-prone individuals and ignores these systemic benefits, as well as the fact that people with very dark skin have almost no risk of sun-induced skin cancer and stand to gain enormously from sun exposure for cardiovascular health.

HRV as a Mortality Biomarker — Specific Studies and Actionable Protocols (Nick Norwitz)

The 1987 American Journal of Cardiology study (n=808, post-MI) found low HRV patients had 5.3x higher all-cause mortality over subsequent years. A separate prospective study (n=2,501, 3.5-year follow-up) found each standard deviation drop in HRV associated with 47% higher risk of a first cardiac event, independent of traditional risk factors. For improvement: Zone 2 (60-70% max HR) is the most robustly supported intervention; HIIT (20 min, 1-2x/week) can amplify gains when fully recovered but suppresses HRV acutely — use your daily HRV reading to time sessions. Omega-3s at 3,000–4,000mg EPA+DHA/day have RCT support for HRV improvement via neuronal membrane fluidity, inflammation reduction, and brain stem vagal tone — a clinically meaningful dose achievable with ~3 cans of sardines daily or targeted supplementation. Measure HRV first thing on waking before caffeine, movement, or phone for a clean baseline; consistency of protocol matters more than which method you use.

Isometric Training Is Less Inferior Than You Think (Stronger by Science)

A deep review of the isometric training literature finds that the conventional wisdom — isometrics are significantly inferior to dynamic training for strength and hypertrophy — is not well-supported by the current evidence base. The review examines the mechanisms and study designs behind the assumption and finds meaningful limitations; isometrics may have a more legitimate role in programming than most trainees give them credit for, particularly for injury contexts or accessory work. No specific protocol recommendations summarized in the excerpt, but worth reading if you're considering isometric holds as a programming tool.

A pointed Stoic argument: separating reason from ethics — treating reason as a morally neutral optimization tool — was always a mistake, and the consequences of doing so are visible everywhere. Useful framing for anyone thinking about AI ethics, decision-making frameworks, or the limits of purely consequentialist reasoning.
