🎥 From People I Follow

Worth your time:

Jared Alderman (GTO Wizard)

Alderman sits down with Charlie, a live poker specialist who argues that the next major frontier in high-stakes poker isn't more solver study — it's live tells and psychological self-awareness. Charlie's thesis: the theoretical edge is shrinking as information democratizes, but the ability to feel an opponent's emotional state, read physical timing cues, and stay psychologically balanced is wide open and undertaught. He makes a sharp distinction between two kinds of live tells: the intuitive "vibe" read (hard to teach, Phil Ivey territory) and pattern-based behavioral tells — "this happened at second 3, then this at second 7, what does that mean for their range?" — which he calls highly teachable and nearly impossible to hide. He also makes the bold claim: "You could put in 100 hours studying theory and make less EV than if you meditated for 10 minutes before every session." Both agree that poker players are more familiar with the feeling of being theoretically outgunned than psychologically outgunned — and that gap is the next edge.

GTO Lab

Jonathan Jaffe and Nick Petrangelo work through a deep-stacked multiway spot from the WSOP 250K against Taylor vonberg, using it to make a broader point: in spots with thin edges, bet sizing tells become critical information that players often fail to integrate when they reach a big river decision. Their core insight is that humans playing high stakes aren't robots — a B33 versus B25 choice on the flop can signal hand strength, and you should trace that sizing information all the way to the river rather than tunnel-visioning on "call or fold?" They also make a nuanced point on multiway play: GTO solves exist but don't reflect how humans actually play, so intuition and hand-reading remain disproportionately valuable in multi-way pots. The WSOP prep framing is practical: focus on what you can control (decisions, attention, energy management), because any single tournament result has high variance regardless.

Nick Norwitz

Two solid pieces. On selenium and neurogenesis: a Cell Metabolism study found that exercise increases the selenium transporter SEPP1 in the brain, which shuttles selenium to generate seleno proteins that reduce oxidative stress and wake dormant neural stem cells in the hippocampus — a 55% increase in neurogenesis in treated mice. A human RCT (n=20, avg age 78, 6 months) found one Brazil nut per day raised blood selenium from 56 to 291 mcg/L and produced statistically significant improvements in verbal fluency and visual-spatial/executive function. One Brazil nut exceeds the daily selenium RDA of 55 mcg. The sardine experiment is a fun data point: after eating ~1,000 sardines in a month, his omega-3 index went off the Omega Quant scale (>12), he became cold-resistant, and his girlfriend could smell fish emanating from his skin even when fasted — suggesting omega-3s were literally exuding through his pores.

Selenium → Neurogenesis Pipeline (Nick Norwitz / Cell Metabolism)

The mechanistic chain is worth internalizing: exercise → increases SEPP1 (selenium transporter) in brain → more selenium → more seleno proteins → less oxidative stress + stem cell activation → new neurons in hippocampus (+55% in treated mice). The human RCT data (one Brazil nut/day, 6 months, n=20 elderly with mild cognitive impairment) showed statistically significant improvements in verbal fluency and visual-spatial function. Practically: one Brazil nut per day clears the selenium RDA; fatty fish (sardines, halibut) provide similar selenium in a more bioavailable amino-acid-bound form plus DHA.

GLP-1 + Myostatin Inhibitor Combination: The Body Recomposition Drug Stack

Mike Israetel (Renaissance Periodization) flagged that myostatin inhibitor drugs are in clinical trials right now, and the anticipated combination therapy with tirzepatide/semaglutide addresses the "Ozempic face" problem directly: GLP-1 drugs suppress appetite but cause muscle loss alongside fat loss; a myostatin blocker preserves or adds muscle during the same cut. The projected outcome of the combo: ~25 lbs of pure fat loss, zero muscle loss, potentially +5-10 lbs of muscle if training and protein are optimized simultaneously. If the trial data holds, this is a category-shifting development for body composition medicine.
