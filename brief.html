Mental Model · Risk/Mathematics

Convexity vs. Concavity

Convex payoffs benefit from volatility (options, bets with capped losses). Concave payoffs are hurt by volatility (leverage, short-term trading). Life structures that are convex compound in your favor; concave structures blow up eventually. Structure your life to be convex where possible.

GTO Wizard

Tom Boss 21 and Joey Ingram walk through the foundational math unifying pot odds, MDF, and bluff frequency — the core insight being that on the river, the pot odds you lay your opponent must equal your bluffing frequency, and vice versa. The video uses GTO Wizard simulations to show how sensitive river decisions are: shifting a villain's bluff frequency by just a few combos flips every bluff-catcher from "always call" to "always fold." The node-lock demonstrations make the indifference concept viscerally real — when the math is right, hands with exactly 37% equity are mixing between calls and folds; tilt it slightly and the entire range snaps to one action. The blocker discussion (why nines are preferred over tens in a specific spot) ties back to scarcity of bluffing combos, not raw hand strength.

Nick Norwitz

Norwitz delivers a well-sourced deep dive on heart rate variability (HRV) as a marker of vagal tone and "neurocardiac agility." Key data: in an 808-patient post-MI study (AJC 1987), low HRV predicted 5.3× higher mortality over subsequent years; in a separate 2,501-person prospective study, each one standard deviation drop in HRV was associated with a 47% increased risk of a first cardiac event. Practically: measure HRV immediately on waking before caffeine or movement for a clean baseline. To improve it — Zone 2 cardio is the most reliable lever (targets 60-70% max HR); HIIT works but must be dosed carefully (he does 2x/week, ~20 min) and your daily HRV reading tells you when you've recovered enough to go again. Omega-3s (EPA+DHA at 3,000–4,000 mg/day, achievable with 3 cans of sardines) have RCT support for boosting HRV via membrane fluidity and vagal tone through brainstem accumulation. Caffeine transiently suppresses HRV but up to 4 cups/day is neutral-to-beneficial long-term.

🧬 Health & Science

Slow Breathing Modulates Brain Function and Risk Behavior — Neuron

A new Cell/Neuron paper found that slow breathing causally modulates brain function and risk-taking behavior, not just physiological state. This is a meaningful mechanistic finding: it suggests the breath→brain relationship operates through identifiable neural circuits, not just vagal tone generally, which has implications for the evidence base around breathwork interventions in stress, decision-making, and emotional regulation. Worth tracking for anyone interested in the neuroscience behind Stoic or mindfulness practices.

Whole Cross-Sectional Human Ultrasound Tomography — Nature Biomedical Engineering

Researchers demonstrated whole cross-sectional ultrasound tomography of the human body — essentially a full-body ultrasound scan without radiation. The capability leap here is the ability to image soft tissue at scale in a way MRI can approximate but with very different cost and access profiles. Early-stage but a potentially significant development for metabolic health monitoring (visceral fat quantification, organ imaging) if it becomes clinically accessible.

Reason Without Virtue Is Just Calculation

Massimo Pigliucci argues that the Enlightenment move to separate reason from ethics was a philosophical error with real consequences — pure instrumental rationality produces optimization, not wisdom, and the Stoic integration of logos (reason) with virtue was more coherent than modernity's cleaner but impoverished separation. A timely essay given how much "rational" discourse around AI alignment, economics, and strategy treats reason as value-neutral.
