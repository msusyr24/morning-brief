Nick Norwitz

Norwitz makes a compelling case that cardiology's LDL fixation misses the point for longevity. PCSK9 loss-of-function mutations dramatically lower LDL but show no lifespan benefit (trend actually goes toward more mortality). By contrast, ApoC3 loss-of-function variants are 2.5x more common in centenarians—despite having virtually identical LDL/ApoB levels. The difference: ApoC3 regulates triglyceride clearance, and lower ApoC3 means better metabolic efficiency. Actionable interventions: ketogenic diets reduced ApoC3 by 50% in 2 weeks in one study; fructose is worse than glucose for ApoC3 production; omega-3s help. Heart rate variability short ties in—low HRV associated with 5.3x higher post-heart attack mortality risk.
