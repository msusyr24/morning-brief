Mental Model · Economics

Supply and Demand

Prices are signals, not values. When something is scarce relative to demand, its price rises until demand drops or supply increases. Most people ignore this when buying bubbles (tulips, crypto) or selling panics (2008 real estate). Munger's defense: always ask, 'relative to what supply, and what demand level?'

GTO Wizard

GTO Wizard launched PLO (Pot-Limit Omaha) solver tools, and Tombos21 walked through the key mindset shifts required coming from Hold'em. The central insight: in PLO, hand strength right now is almost irrelevant — what matters is your hand's nut potential by the river, plus future blockers. A striking concrete finding: when facing a 4-bet in a 100bb cash game, 86% of the 4-betting range is aces, which means KK is often a fold in that spot — counterintuitive to any Hold'em player. Tombos also highlighted that PLO features far more calling and less 3-betting preflop than Hold'em because equities run much closer, and being in position to see cheap multiway flops is more valuable than bloating the pot OOP.

Peter Attia (with Layne Norton)

This segment drills into whether industrial processing — not just linoleic acid itself — is what makes seed oils harmful. Norton argues the extraction process (hexane washing, steam evaporation) is shorter and lower-temperature than what's required to cause meaningful oxidation, and that residual hexane levels in finished oils are typically 0.05–0.5 ppm — far below any threshold associated with harm, especially since hexane's toxicity is primarily from inhalation, not ingestion. His back-of-envelope: to get mild side effects from hexane in seed oil, you'd need to consume ~11,340 kg of oil at once. The more substantive open question he raises but doesn't fully close: does lifetime cumulative low-level exposure to processing byproducts contribute to chronic disease — and the honest answer is we don't have definitive data either way.

🧬 Health & Science

CRISPR Liver-Targeted Gene Editing Drops LDL 62% in Humans

A clinical study used CRISPR packaged in lipid nanoparticles programmed to target only liver cells — not brain, heart, muscle, or germline — achieving a one-time 62% LDL reduction. As Nick Norwitz argues, the cholesterol headline undersells the real advance: tissue-specific delivery of gene editing tools is the breakthrough, opening potential pathways for Alzheimer's (brain-targeted), cancer (immune cell engineering), and sarcopenia (muscle preservation). The ethical and unknown long-term safety questions are real and unresolved.

Nick Norwitz

Isometric Training: The Evidence Is Better Than You Think

Stronger by Science's review finds that the assumption isometrics are inferior to dynamic training for hypertrophy and strength is not well-supported by the current literature. Key moderating variable: muscle length at the isometric contraction point — isometrics performed at long muscle lengths appear particularly effective. This has real programming implications for anyone dealing with joint stress or looking to add intensity variation.

Stronger by Science

Seed Oil Processing: The Hexane Question Examined

Layne Norton on Peter Attia's podcast provided the most quantitative treatment of the hexane-in-seed-oils question I've seen: finished oils contain 0.05–0.5 ppm residual hexane (often non-detectable), hexane toxicity is primarily via inhalation not ingestion, and rodent lethality studies require human-equivalent doses that would require consuming over 11,000 kg of oil. The honest open question: chronic lifetime low-level exposure to processing byproducts and whether that contributes to systemic inflammation — and this genuinely hasn't been studied with the rigor the question deserves.

Peter Attia / Layne Norton

Sardine Nutrition: What Six Tins a Day Actually Delivers

Dr. Sten Ekberg's five-day sardine experiment produced some useful concrete numbers: ~5,000mg EPA+DHA/day (vs. 80–100mg average American intake and 2,000–4,000mg therapeutic target), 1,600mg calcium as hydroxyapatite (the same crystalline form as human bone, absorbed at any pH — contrasted with calcium carbonate supplements like Tums, which require the stomach acid they simultaneously neutralize). The 5-lb weight loss over 5 days and the fatigue he reported are attributed primarily to not being fat-adapted beforehand and running a ~1,000–1,400 cal/day deficit.

How Anyone Can Think Like a Roman Emperor

Donald Robertson on Stoicism and what he calls "Majesty as a State of Mind" — the idea that Marcus Aurelius used his role not as a source of pride but as a constant reminder of the gap between how a ruler should act and how he actually felt. The frame: adopting the perspective of your highest aspired self as a practical cognitive tool, not a flattering self-image. Short but the kind of thing worth sitting with.

How to Cope with Boethius

Massimo Pigliucci on Boethius's Consolation of Philosophy — written while awaiting execution — as a practical manual for dealing with radical reversals of fortune. The core Boethian argument: Fortune's gifts (wealth, power, status) were never really "yours" to begin with, so their removal is not a loss of something that belonged to you. Pairs well with Stoic reading; Boethius bridges classical Stoicism and later philosophical traditions on adversity.
