🎥 From People I Follow

Also posted:

  • Ben Felix: Argues that money's effect on happiness is surprisingly weak — the correlation between log income and happiness is only 0.09, and the difference in experienced happiness between households earning $15k and $250k is about 5 points on a 100-point scale. The real drivers of a good life are the PERMA-V model factors (positive emotion, engagement, relationships, meaning, accomplishment, vitality), and people systematically overestimate how much higher income will improve their wellbeing. Key practical takeaway: prioritizing time over money — even at the cost of income — empirically leads to greater happiness, better relationships, and more meaningful work. (Using Your Money To Be Happier)
  • Lex Fridman: Deep dive with FFmpeg/VLC creators Jean-Baptiste Kempf and Kieran Kunhya on the invisible infrastructure behind all internet video — FFmpeg underlies YouTube, Netflix, Chrome, and essentially 90%+ of video processing; VLC has been downloaded 6.5B+ times. Notable: video compression achieves 100–1000x reduction by exploiting human perceptual limits (luminance vs. color, temporal redundancy), and FFmpeg contains 100,000+ lines of hand-written assembly because compilers still can't match it. JB recounts refusing tens of millions of dollars in acquisition offers to keep VLC ad-free, and the remarkable process of tracking down 350+ contributors (including a factory worker whose deceased son had contributed code) to re-license VLC from GPL to LGPL. (FFmpeg: The Incredible Technology Behind Video on the Internet)

Senate Confirms Kevin Warsh as Fed Chair

The Senate has confirmed Kevin Warsh — described as Bitcoin-friendly — as the new Federal Reserve chair, a significant shift from the Powell era. This comes one day before a scheduled Senate vote on the CLARITY Act, which Coinbase CEO Brian Armstrong claims could "fundamentally reshape U.S. finance" by establishing a regulatory framework for digital assets. A Warsh-led Fed is expected to be more receptive to sound money principles and potentially less hostile to Bitcoin as a reserve asset.

This is a meaningful distribution milestone: Schwab's retail reach dwarfs most crypto-native platforms, and spot access (not just ETFs) signals a new phase of Bitcoin's integration into traditional finance.

🧬 Health & Science

Hold Still: What Does the Evidence Actually Say About Isometric Training for Strength and Hypertrophy?

Stronger By Science's deep dive challenges the conventional wisdom that isometric (static contraction) training is inferior to dynamic training for building strength and muscle. The review examines current evidence on mechanisms, joint-angle specificity of strength gains, and hypertrophy outcomes from isometric protocols — arguing the gap between iso and dynamic training is smaller than commonly assumed and that isometrics may have underappreciated use cases, particularly for pain management and rehab contexts. Worth reading if you program around joint limitations or are curious about non-traditional modalities with actual mechanistic backing.

Stronger By Science

Heritability of Human Lifespan is ~50% When Heritability is Redefined

A reanalysis arguing that prior estimates of lifespan heritability (~25%) were deflated because traditional methods count shared environment as non-genetic — but when you separate genetic from environmental factors more carefully, the heritable component of how long you live may be closer to 50%. The implication: your genes matter substantially more for longevity than the oft-cited "lifestyle is everything" framing suggests, though this doesn't negate the large role of modifiable factors.
