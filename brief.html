🧬 Health & Science

GLP-1 Muscle Loss Is Probably Overblown — Here's the Mechanism

From Nick Norwitz's deep dive: the claim that GLP-1s cause excessive muscle loss relative to diet-only interventions is being revised. The key mechanism — DEXA scans cannot distinguish contractile muscle proteins from liver mass and non-functional intracellular substrates. A 2026 study suggests GLP-1s preferentially reduce liver size (not a bad outcome) and non-essential muscle components, while human studies show strength can be fully preserved despite falling lean-mass numbers on DEXA. Practical implication: the commonly cited "25-40% of weight lost is lean mass" figure is likely inflating the muscle concern by lumping shrinking livers into the lean-mass category. Rule of thumb for mitigating real muscle loss: resistance train 3x/week, consume 0.7-1g protein per pound of lean mass during the weight-loss phase.

Nick Norwitz — Should Everyone Take GLP-1s?

HIIT Outperforms Zone 2 for Hippocampal Preservation — Effect Lasts 5 Years

From the Tommy Wood episode: an RCT in older adults comparing low-intensity exercise, zone-2 treadmill work (3x/week), and the Norwegian 4×4 HIIT protocol (4 min at 85-95% max HR × 4 sets, 3x/week for 6 months). Fitness improvements were equal between zone-2 and HIIT groups, but the HIIT group showed significantly better hippocampal function and structural preservation on MRI — and that benefit persisted for 5 years after the 6-month intervention ended. This is a meaningful finding for anyone thinking about exercise selection for long-term brain health, not just cardiovascular fitness.

Huberman Lab — Dr. Tommy Wood

HOMA-IR: Why Normal Blood Glucose Badly Understates Insulin Resistance

From Sten Ekberg: the HOMA-IR formula (fasting glucose × fasting insulin ÷ 405) reveals that blood glucose is a controlled variable — the body works hard to keep it stable, so it's a lagging indicator. A person with glucose of 90 and insulin of 4.5 has a HOMA-IR of 1.0 (healthy). A person with glucose of 92 and insulin of 15 has a HOMA-IR of 3.4 — 3.4× more insulin resistant — yet the glucose numbers look nearly identical. Fasting insulin is almost never tested in standard panels but is the most informative early signal. Uric acid is another early marker that shifts before lipids or glucose do, and is easy to add to standard bloodwork.

Dr. Sten Ekberg — 10 Signs You're Becoming Insulin Resistant
