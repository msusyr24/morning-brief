Mental Model · Risk/Mathematics

Convexity vs. Concavity

Convex payoffs benefit from volatility (options, bets with capped losses). Concave payoffs are hurt by volatility (leverage, short-term trading). Life structures that are convex compound in your favor; concave structures blow up eventually. Structure your life to be convex where possible.

🧬 Health & Science

GLP-1 Muscle Loss: The DEXA Problem

Nick Norwitz covers a Cell Reports Medicine study clarifying that the widely-cited "40% lean mass loss" figure for GLP-1 drugs overstates functional muscle loss because DEXA scans bundle liver mass, intramuscular glycogen and lipid substrates, and actual contractile muscle tissue into one "lean mass" number. When isolated, functional muscle strength in both upper and lower body is fully maintained even as size slightly decreases — suggesting the drugs are clearing metabolic "junk" from muscle rather than degrading contractile tissue. This meaningfully reframes the GLP-1 + resistance training conversation: the concern should shift from "will I lose muscle?" to "am I training hard enough to preserve and build on top of the baseline?"

Nick Norwitz — New GLP-1 Data Just Changed the Muscle Loss Story

Seed Oil Processing: What Actually Matters?

The Attia/Norton exchange gets specific on hexane extraction: residue levels in finished seed oils are typically 0.05–0.5 ppm, hexane toxicity is an inhalation (not ingestion) risk, and the dose required for any physiological harm via consumption is effectively unreachable. Norton's position: the processing argument is largely a red herring — the real question remains whether linoleic acid concentration itself (now ~10% of food availability vs. ~3% a century ago) is the operative variable. Attia doesn't fully concede, raising the chronic-exposure / slow-disease-development concern as a gap in the evidence. The honest takeaway: the hexane-as-poison argument has weak footing, but the broader seed oil debate remains genuinely unsettled.

Peter Attia — Is Industrial Processing the Real Problem With Seed Oils?

Prostate Cancer: The Case for Aggressive PSA Screening

Peter Attia argues that the tools to catch prostate cancer early already exist but are systematically underused — the piece makes the case for proactive PSA monitoring rather than waiting for symptoms or following conservative screening guidelines that were calibrated around population averages rather than individual risk profiles.

Peter Attia — Prostate cancer: a PSA on PSA

Hyperinsulinemia as Root Cause of Common Skin Conditions

Ken Berry makes a mechanistically grounded case that seven skin conditions — keratosis pilaris, skin tags, acanthosis nigricans, hidradenitis suppurativa, acne, rosacea, and eczema/psoriasis — are downstream of chronically elevated insulin rather than genetics or bad luck. The diagnostic lever: fasting insulin (not just fasting glucose or HbA1c), with a target under 10 µIU/mL and ideally around 5. He argues dermatologists and PCPs routinely miss this because fasting insulin isn't standard in metabolic panels, and that carbohydrate reduction — sufficient to lower fasting insulin below 10 — often produces dramatic improvement or full remission in weeks to months. He personally resolved stage-1 rosacea this way.

Ken Berry MD — 7 Skin Signs of Insulin Resistance
