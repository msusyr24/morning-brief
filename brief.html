🧬 Health & Science

Hold Still: What Does the Evidence Actually Say About Isometric Training for Strength and Hypertrophy?

Stronger by Science's deep dive challenges the conventional dismissal of isometric (static contraction) training. The review finds that isometrics produce comparable hypertrophy to dynamic training when effort and volume are equated, and can produce strength gains — though with a well-known angle-specificity caveat (strength gains are most pronounced ±15° of the trained joint angle). Practical implications: isometrics may be underutilized for rehab contexts, tendon loading, and situations where full ROM is contraindicated. The article also covers where isometrics genuinely underperform: they're likely inferior for developing strength across a full range of motion and for sports requiring dynamic force expression.

Stronger by Science

Decades of Sleep Research Produces a New Pharmacological Treatment for Sleep Apnea

A new drug (tirzepatide, also used for metabolic disease) has shown meaningful efficacy in reducing sleep apnea severity in clinical trials, with the mechanism likely running through weight loss and upper airway muscle tone rather than a direct respiratory effect. This is notable because CPAP remains the standard but has ~50% long-term non-adherence — a pharmacological option that actually works would be a real clinical shift. The University of Toronto researchers involved trace the mechanistic story back through decades of basic sleep science.

How General Semantics Was Used to Treat WWII Veterans

Donald Robertson traces how Douglas Kelley — the psychiatrist who evaluated Nuremberg defendants — used General Semantics (a precursor to CBT and rational-emotive therapy) to treat combat trauma in the 1940s. The practical insight: helping veterans distinguish between their linguistic/cognitive maps and reality reduced catastrophizing and emotional reactivity — essentially a proto-Stoic technique deployed clinically decades before CBT was formalized. Robertson draws the line directly to modern Stoic therapy practice.
