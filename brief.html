Mental Model · Risk/Mathematics

Convexity vs. Concavity

Convex payoffs benefit from volatility (options, bets with capped losses). Concave payoffs are hurt by volatility (leverage, short-term trading). Life structures that are convex compound in your favor; concave structures blow up eventually. Structure your life to be convex where possible.

GTO Wizard / Jared Alderman

A genuinely interesting podcast on poker's next edge: the guest (Charlie, a high-stakes tournament crusher) argues that theoretical GTO knowledge is now so commoditized that the real exploitable gap is psychological self-knowledge and live reads — specifically, knowing what it feels like to be your opponent in a given moment and using that to collapse their range. He distinguishes two categories of live tells: (1) intuitive "vibe-reading" à la Phil Ivey, which is hard to teach, and (2) systematic behavioral pattern recognition — specific timing and physical tells that he says are "extremely teachable" and that most of the poker world doesn't know exist. His thesis: within 5 years, the high-stakes community will widely adopt live tell frameworks, and within 15 years, the psychological/mindfulness layer will follow. He also makes the point that poker is a parallel problem-solving environment, not a linear one — so "improving your thought process" is the wrong frame; the skill is more like pattern synthesis, closer to art than algorithm.

Nick Norwitz

Norwitz makes a case for ApoC3 as a more actionable longevity biomarker than LDL or ApoB. The key data point: centenarians (100+ year olds) were 2.5× more likely to carry a loss-of-function ApoC3 genetic variant — and the longevity benefit was not mediated by LDL, which was essentially unchanged. In a meta-analysis of 137,895 individuals, ApoC3 loss-of-function reduced vascular disease risk while LDL dropped only 4%, but remnant cholesterol dropped 43%. The actionable lifestyle levers he cites: low-carb/ketogenic diets (one study showed 50% ApoC3 reduction in 2 weeks in obese subjects); fructose restriction (fructose appears to be a stronger ApoC3 stimulus than glucose); omega-3s (EPA/DHA, targeting an omega-3 index ≥8%); and improving insulin sensitivity via any means. He explicitly flags that PCSK9 loss-of-function — which dramatically lowers LDL — shows no lifespan benefit and trends toward slightly higher mortality, making the contrast with ApoC3 genetics striking.

🧬 Health & Science

ApoC3: The Longevity Lipid Marker Most Doctors Haven't Heard Of

Nick Norwitz's breakdown (summarized above in YouTube) is the substantive health science item today. Key findings worth sitting with: centenarians carry loss-of-function ApoC3 variants at 2.5× the rate of shorter-lived controls; the longevity signal is independent of LDL/ApoB, which barely moves; and the differentiating lipid markers are higher HDL and lower triglyceride-to-HDL ratio — classic metabolic health signals. The most actionable lever with the strongest evidence: ketogenic diet reduced ApoC3 by ~50% in obese subjects in just 2 weeks. Fructose restriction appears to be the single highest-leverage dietary move, since fructose stimulates hepatic ApoC3 production more potently than glucose.

Prostate Cancer: A PSA on PSA

Peter Attia's piece argues that PSA-based screening is underused despite being one of the few available tools for early prostate cancer detection — and that the historical overreaction to false positives led to systematic under-screening that costs lives. The core claim: we have the tools to catch this early, and the medical establishment's risk-aversion around PSA has overcorrected.

Landmark Ancient-Genome Study Shows Surprise Acceleration of Human Evolution

A large ancient-DNA study finds that human evolution accelerated more recently than previously understood — specifically in the last few thousand years, not just in the deep past. The finding challenges the assumption that evolutionary pressures on humans have largely relaxed since the agricultural transition.
