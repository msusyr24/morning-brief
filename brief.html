🧬 Health & Science

Hold Still: What Does the Evidence Actually Say About Isometric Training for Strength and Hypertrophy?

Stronger by Science digs into isometric training — holds and static contractions — and finds the conventional wisdom ("it's inferior for strength and size") isn't well supported by current evidence. The article examines mechanisms (motor unit recruitment, time under tension, joint-angle specificity of strength gains) and what the data actually shows about hypertrophy outcomes compared to dynamic training. Worth reading if you've dismissed isometrics or are looking for training variety with real physiological grounding.

Stronger by Science

A Critical Look at Ultra-Processed Diets and Male Reproductive Health

Attia's team examines a controlled feeding study in young men looking at ultra-processed food intake and reproductive health markers. The framing is deliberately cautious — the piece focuses on what a controlled feeding study can and cannot tell us, which is itself a useful lesson in reading diet research. Worth reading for the methodology critique as much as the findings.

Genetic Testing: When It's Valuable, How to Choose the Right Test, What to Do with Results

Attia's episode #392 takes a sober look at genetic testing in medicine — arguing its value "depends almost entirely on how thoughtfully it is used." Where Did the Stoic God Come From?

Massimo Pigliucci traces the theological foundations of Stoicism back through ancient Greek philosophy — specifically the lineage from Heraclitus through Zeno to the Stoic concept of the Logos as a rational, immanent divine principle. Understanding this is useful because Stoic ethics are deeply entangled with their metaphysics; knowing the roots helps you evaluate what survives contact with a modern, non-theistic worldview.

Agora-1: The Multi-Agent World Model

Odyssey is releasing Agora-1, a world model designed for multi-agent environments — meaning an AI that builds and reasons about a persistent, simulated model of the world rather than just responding to prompts. Anthropic and the Gates Foundation are partnering at $200M to deploy Claude in global health and development contexts — disease surveillance, healthcare access in low-resource settings, agricultural productivity.
