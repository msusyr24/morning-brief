GTO Wizard

First-ever GTO Wizard PLO livestream with Tombos21 and Joey Ingram, walking through the new PLO solver tool. Key insight: in PLO, pre-flop strategy skews heavily toward calling rather than 3-betting — even in spots where NLH players would use a pure 3b/fold strategy — because equities run much closer and out-of-position pot inflation is punished harder. Tombos highlighted a striking GTO finding: in a HJ vs SB 4-bet pot, 86% of the 4-betting range is pocket aces, making hands like KK nearly always correct folds. The central PLO mindset shift discussed: hand strength at the current street is largely irrelevant — what matters is nut potential by the river, plus blocker utility for future bluffing opportunities. Joey and Tombos also walked through how check-leading ranges differ sharply from NLH (you have wider leading ranges vs. aggressor in PLO), and how back-door draws and future blockers define red-line winrate.

🧬 Health & Science

Hold Still: What Does the Evidence Actually Say About Isometric Training?

Stronger by Science reviews the evidence on isometric (static contraction) training for strength and hypertrophy, challenging the conventional dismissal of it as inferior to dynamic training. The article argues current evidence doesn't support the assumption that full range-of-motion dynamic training is clearly superior — isometrics produce meaningful strength and size adaptations, particularly at the trained joint angle, and may have underappreciated application for injury rehab and programming variety. Worth reading for the actual study breakdown rather than the headline claim.

Sleep Pharmacology: Medications, Emerging Therapies, and Supplement Evidence

Peter Attia episode with a sleep pharmacologist matching drug pharmacokinetics to specific sleep problems — sleep onset vs. sleep maintenance insomnia require different agents with different half-lives. The framing that pharmacology must match the patient's specific challenge (not just "take a sleep aid") is the core clinical principle. Evidence on common OTC supplements (magnesium, melatonin, glycine) also covered.

Bimagrumab Phase 2: 100% Fat Loss, Simultaneous Muscle Gain

A Nature Medicine 2026 paper on bimagrumab — a monoclonal antibody targeting myostatin signaling — found that obese participants lost weight with 100% of the loss coming from fat mass while simultaneously gaining muscle and grip strength, with no increase in physical activity. This is mechanistically distinct from GLP-1 agonists (which cause ~25-39% lean mass loss alongside fat loss). The drug is a partial mimic of the myostatin gene mutation seen in double-muscled cattle and a handful of humans. Phase 2 complete; not yet approved. Side effects were mild GI and headaches. (Via Nick Norwitz's video.)

The Unreasonable Redundancy of Nature's Protein Folds

A biology research post examining why nature uses a surprisingly small number of protein fold architectures repeatedly across vastly different functions — a phenomenon that turns out to have deep implications for how we think about protein evolution and the feasibility of protein design. The "unreasonable redundancy" framing parallels Wigner's "unreasonable effectiveness of mathematics" and is genuinely thought-provoking for anyone following AlphaFold-era structural biology.
