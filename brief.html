Nick Norwitz

Norwitz presents ApoC3 (apolipoprotein C-III) as a potentially more important longevity marker than LDL cholesterol, citing data that centenarians are 2.5x more likely to carry genetic variants that reduce ApoC3, despite having similar or even slightly higher ApoB levels. He argues this explains a key paradox: PCSK9 loss-of-function mutations dramatically lower LDL but provide no lifespan benefit (if anything trending toward higher mortality), while ApoC3 loss-of-function improves cardiovascular outcomes and longevity without meaningfully changing LDL. ApoC3 acts as a "metabolic brake" that slows triglyceride clearance; lowering it improves metabolic efficiency and insulin sensitivity. His evidence-based intervention recommendations: ketogenic/low-carb diets (which reduced ApoC3 by 50% in 2 weeks in one obesity study), restricting fructose specifically (a stronger ApoC3 stimulus than glucose), increasing omega-3 fatty acids to an 8% red blood cell index, and improving insulin sensitivity through any means. Separately, he flagged heart rate variability (HRV) as predicting 5.3x higher mortality risk post-heart attack and 50% higher heart attack risk in healthy populations.

GTO Wizard

GTO Wizard built a comprehensive benchmark testing whether large language models can actually play poker competently, measuring them against GTO Wizard AI using AIVAT (a variance-reduction technique pioneered by CFR researchers). Results were brutal: the best performer, GPT-5.3, still lost 16.3 bb/100 (versus ~5 bb/100 for top human experts), while some models like Grok-4 lost at nearly 60 bb/100—barely better than folding every hand. LLMs showed systematic weaknesses: nitty preflop ranges (defending big blind with only premium three-bets instead of linear suited/connected ranges), frequent hand misreads (calling queen-nine offsuit "queen-nine suited"), and a tendency to make excessive hero calls with weak holdings. The benchmark is now public with an API for developers to test their own models. On the strategy side, Nick Petrangelo emphasized that understanding equilibrium check-raise frequencies is essential before exploiting—you can't know how much to deviate without knowing the correct baseline amount.

🧬 Health & Science

All 12 Moonwalkers Had "Lunar Hay Fever" From Gunpowder-Smelling Dust

Every Apollo astronaut who walked on the Moon experienced what they called "lunar hay fever"—sneezing, watery eyes, and sore throats triggered by Moon dust that smells like gunpowder. The fine, electrostatically charged lunar regolith clung to spacesuits and was carried into the lunar modules, where it became a persistent irritant. The mechanism likely involves the dust's sharp, abrasive particles (never weathered by wind or water) and possible chemical reactions with the cabin's oxygen-rich atmosphere. This remains a significant engineering and health challenge for future long-duration lunar missions.
