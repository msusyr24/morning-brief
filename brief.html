GTO Wizard / GTO Lab

GTO Wizard's PLO video makes a strong case for the SIP method (Simplify, Isolate, Pattern): instead of memorizing solver frequencies combo-by-combo, build heuristics from recurring structural patterns. The core insight is that simplified strategies executed well beat complex strategies executed poorly — and in PLO specifically, the EV cost of simplification is small while the execution benefit is large. Practical takeaways: use "when in doubt, open" for BTN, "when in doubt, fold" for EP; treat mixed spots as threshold problems (find the cutoff between pure call and pure fold rather than trying to mix); on locked boards (paired/monotone/straight-heavy), use small sizing (1/4–1/3); on regular boards, default to half-pot. GTO Lab's short adds a complementary exploitative point: on ace-high boards, population calls the same range regardless of whether you size 33% or 40%+, so sizing up is a low-risk exploit since villain can't distinguish sizes and won't adjust their calling range.

🧬 Health & Science

Antioxidant Supplements: The ORAC Framework Was Wrong

Dr. Sten Ekberg's video synthesizes a body of RCT evidence that antioxidant supplements don't just fail to help — they can actively harm. The FDA quietly removed ORAC values from its databases in 2012. The mechanism: free radicals are intracellular signals that activate NRF2, which triggers the body's own catalase/SOD/glutathione system (capable of neutralizing millions of radicals per molecule, vs. the 1-for-1 ratio of supplement antioxidants). Flooding the system externally suppresses that signal. The two most damning data points: beta-carotene supplementation showed a 28% increase in cancer across two stopped-early trials; a meta-analysis of 68 RCTs (230,000 people) found beta-carotene, vitamin A, and vitamin E supplements all increased mortality. Exception: R-alpha lipoic acid, which recycles endogenous antioxidants rather than displacing them. Separately, isolated alpha-tocopherol (the standard vitamin E pill) actively competes with and depletes gamma-tocopherol — the other critical form — making supplementation counterproductive even if antioxidants were useful.

Dr. Sten Ekberg

Watermelon as a Performance and Recovery Tool: Actual Mechanisms

Thomas DeLauer's video covers real data: a Food and Nutrition Research study on 21 amateur runners found that 500ml of watermelon juice pre-race kept plasma lactate significantly lower (more aerobic at the same effort), preserved jump performance post-race while placebo group dropped 9–10%, and produced 95% zero-soreness at 72 hrs vs. 67% placebo. The mechanisms are citrulline → arginine → nitric oxide (vasodilation + ammonia clearance via urea cycle) and a glucose-delivery pathway during exercise that bypasses insulin signaling entirely (contraction-activated GLUT4 translocation via calcium/AMPK/NO). Practical: eat flesh near the rind (highest citrulline concentration), add sea salt (sodium is the strongest individual driver of fluid retention), and consider it during exercise as an insulin-independent carb source — particularly relevant for insulin-resistant individuals.

Fasting + Chemotherapy: The Differential Killing Mechanism

Peter Attia's short clip articulates the core logic clearly: the challenge in cancer treatment is never killing cancer cells (easy) — it's differential killing. Fasting ≥24 hrs shifts healthy cells into maintenance/repair mode (reduced chemo sensitivity) while tumor cells, unable to downregulate growth due to mutated tumor suppressors, become relatively more vulnerable. Clinical trials have shown water-only fasting reduces chemo side effects, which in practice means patients can tolerate higher doses and complete more treatment cycles — itself a meaningful survival lever.

Marcus Aurelius Was Not an Emotionless Stoic

Donald Robertson (the leading biographer of Marcus Aurelius) makes the case that the popular image of Stoic emotional detachment is a misreading — Marcus grieved, feared, and felt frustration acutely, and the Meditations are evidence of active psychological struggle, not serene transcendence. The distinction matters practically: Stoicism as a toolkit for processing emotion is very different from Stoicism as a prescription for suppression.
