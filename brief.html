🧬 Health & Science

Isometric Training: The Evidence Is Better Than You Think

The Stronger by Science review finds that isometric training produces strength and hypertrophy gains comparable to dynamic training when matched for effort and volume — the oft-cited limitation of poor carryover to other joint angles is real but overstated, and can be largely mitigated by training at longer muscle lengths. Practically: isometrics are underutilized in hypertrophy programming and have strong evidence for tendon health and rehab contexts. The piece also flags that the "isometrics are for rehabilitation only" reputation is largely a historical artifact of low-intensity protocols, not a fundamental limitation of the modality.

Stronger by Science

LDL in Lean Mass Hyper-Responders: Peter Attia's Position

Attia argues directly against the "lipid energy model" being used to justify ignoring dramatically elevated LDL in carnivore/low-carb dieters who are lean and metabolically healthy otherwise. His core claim: the causal evidence from Mendelian randomization and statin/PCSK9 trials is strong enough that no metabolic marker profile exempts someone from LDL-P-driven atherosclerosis risk. The piece is specifically aimed at the LMHR community using good HDL and low triglycerides as a shield — Attia says that's not a safe gamble given current evidence.

Peter Attia MD

Memory Consolidation: Take Your Stimulants After Learning, Not Before

Huberman's Essentials episode on memory (based on McGaugh and Cahill's research) makes a counterintuitive but well-supported point: adrenaline/norepinephrine released after a learning episode is what stamps memories down, not before or during. This means caffeine, alpha-GPC, or other stimulants are more effective for retention when taken late in or immediately after a study/practice session. The mechanism: emotional salience and one-trial learning (conditioned place preference/aversion) both work via post-event adrenaline, and that same window can be deliberately exploited for non-emotional information.

Huberman Lab Essentials

Protein Powder Contamination: 47% Exceed California Lead Limits

Plant-based proteins consistently show higher heavy metal contamination (lead, cadmium) than whey, largely because metals bioaccumulate in crops and concentrate further during protein extraction. Chocolate-flavored products add cadmium risk from cacao. The practical advice: prioritize whey concentrate from pasture-raised sources (also retains beneficial bioactives like lactoferrin that isolate processing removes), look for brands with third-party batch-level testing (Clean Label Project QR codes), and treat "natural flavors" with more scrutiny in the US than in EU-manufactured products where the standard is stricter.

Socrates and AI — Massimo Pigliucci

Pigliucci makes a careful case that LLMs have a Socratic structure — they surface and organize existing knowledge through dialogue rather than generating genuine insight — and explores whether that's a feature or a limitation. He distinguishes the Socratic role (midwife to ideas already latent in the interlocutor) from genuine philosophical inquiry, and asks whether a system that never truly doesn't know something can practice the kind of intellectual humility Socrates modeled. Compact and worth sitting with if you're thinking about how to use AI tools in your own thinking.

What Marcus Aurelius Can Teach Us About Coping with Stress

Donald Robertson's conversation with Mark Forstater covers the practical Stoic framework Marcus used in the Meditations — particularly the discipline of desire (wanting only what is in your control), the view from above (zooming out to see your situation in cosmological scale), and the use of memento mori not as morbidity but as a clarifying lens on what actually matters. Robertson is one of the better modern interpreters of Stoicism who stays close to the original texts rather than diluting it into generic positivity.
