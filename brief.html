Mental Model · Psychology

Reciprocity Tendency

When someone does you a small favor, you feel pressure to return it — often with interest. Hare Krishnas used this by giving flowers at airports before asking for donations. The cost of a flower vs. the guilt of not reciprocating was wildly asymmetric. Aware of this tendency, you can watch for it in negotiations, sales pitches, and favors-exchanged-for-favors politics.

GTO Lab

The WSOP bootcamp preview with Jonathan Jaffe and Leon Sturm is the meatiest content this cycle. The central hand analysis: Kabrhel pots it on the flop with aces against Kanit's 76s at ~5:1 SPR. Jaffe's key insight is that the overbet size itself is information — by choosing pot instead of ~half-pot, Kabrhel signals he doesn't respect Kanit's ability to check-raise bluff, and future-Kanit should exploit that by recognizing Kabrhel will skip the bet when he's vulnerable. On calling vs. raising with the overpair, Jaffe argues the downside of folding out Kabrhel's semi-bluffs (J10s, QJ) outweighs the upside of guaranteeing stacks go in, since most of the time you're getting it in anyway. Separately, the shorts touch on two strong tournament mindset points: (1) know your actual goal for a specific bullet — ROI vs. hourly vs. bracelet value — because optimal strategy differs; (2) the bubble factor doubles on the stone bubble (8.5% → 16.7% risk premium in the example), meaning shoves get through far more often, which is mechanically why chip leaders have so much leverage there.

What Does Bitcoin "Power Projection" Mean to the U.S. Military?

INDOPACOM Commander Admiral Samuel Paparo told Congress the U.S. military is actively running a live Bitcoin node and views the protocol as a "valuable computer science tool" for power projection — language directly echoing Bitcoin advocate Jason Lowery's thesis about Bitcoin as a digital energy-projection mechanism. This is the first instance of a sitting combatant commander publicly framing Bitcoin in strategic military terms before Congress, which is a meaningful signal regardless of where the policy goes from here.

🧬 Health & Science

Hold Still: What Does the Evidence Actually Say About Isometric Training for Strength and Hypertrophy?

Stronger By Science does a full evidence review on isometric training, challenging the conventional wisdom that it's inferior to dynamic training for strength and size. The actual research doesn't cleanly support that claim — isometrics produce meaningful strength and hypertrophy gains, with the main caveat being joint-angle specificity (strength gains are most pronounced at or near the training angle). Practically relevant for anyone with joint issues that limit range of motion, or looking to add intensity without additional mechanical load.
