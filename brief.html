🎥 From People I Follow

Worth your time:

GTO Wizard

GTO Wizard benchmarked major LLMs against their purpose-built poker AI in heads-up play. The results are brutal: GPT-5.3 performed "best" but still lost at 16.3 big blinds per 100 hands—over 3x worse than top human experts (who lose around 5 bb/100 against GTO). Models like GPT-4o and Grok 4 were barely better than folding every hand. The key insight: LLMs are trained to predict language from poker books and forums, while GTO Wizard AI was trained on billions of self-played hands to predict expected value directly. There's a cost-performance curve where spending more on inference helps up to a point, then flatlines—throwing money at reasoning tokens doesn't overcome the fundamental architecture mismatch.

Ken Berry MD

Berry identifies seven skin conditions linked to hyperinsulinemia—keratosis pilaris (chicken skin), skin tags, and rosacea among them. His claim: these are typically dismissed as genetic or bad luck, but chronic high insulin is the real driver, and they often clear within weeks of dietary intervention. He walks through his "proper human diet" protocol: remove added sugars, grains, beans, and seed oils; fill at least half the plate with fatty meat and eggs; use animal fats. Promises to share specific lab markers to test for hyperinsulinemia at video's end.
