Renaissance Periodization (Dr. Mike Israetel)

The standout video makes a strong case against "gaintaining" (eating at maintenance to recomp) using a new study by Vargas Molina et al.: over 10 weeks, a deficit group lost twice as much fat as the maintenance group (6 lb vs. 3 lb) while gaining the same 2 lb of muscle. The core insight is asymmetry: fat loss without muscle loss is easy at moderate body fat levels, but muscle gain is slow regardless of calories — so you lose nothing by cutting aggressively, and gain extra time to bulk. Dr. Mike's recommended protocol: slow bulk (~0.5 lb/week for 12–20 weeks), optional 2-week maintenance bridge, then a 4–8 week cut at ~1 lb/week. Gaintaining still works — especially for beginners or people who can't track — but bulking and cutting is the Corvette vs. the Chevy Malibu. Separately, he updated his position on full ROM: lengthened partial reps (bottom-range only) consistently outperform full ROM for hypertrophy in the research, using calves as the clearest example — failure at lockout ends the set early, but continuing with bottom-range partials after that point drives additional growth.

🧬 Health & Science

Is the Correlation Between Hypertrophy and Strength Gains Stronger Than We Realized?

Stronger by Science digs into whether muscle growth drives more of the strength gains in new lifters than previously thought. The finding: prior studies likely underestimated the hypertrophy-to-strength relationship in novices because they couldn't measure muscle growth precisely enough, but the correlation is still far from 1:1 — neural adaptations remain a major independent factor. Worth reading if you're trying to decide how much of a beginner's strength gains to attribute to size vs. skill.

Stronger by Science

Should you choose organic food?

Peter Attia's team reviews the evidence across pesticide residues, GMO safety, nutrient content differences, and antibiotic exposure from conventional animal products. Worth reading for a structured look at where the data actually supports paying the premium (antibiotic resistance in animal products is the strongest case) vs. where it's mostly marketing (nutrient differences in produce are negligible).

Two masks, one stage — Stoic role ethics from Panaetius to Epictetus

Massimo Pigliucci traces the Stoic doctrine of role ethics from the middle Stoa (Panaetius) through Epictetus: we each play multiple roles simultaneously — our universal nature as rational beings, and our particular social roles (parent, citizen, professional) — and living well means playing each role well without letting one crowd out the others. A useful framework for thinking about identity conflicts and integrity under pressure.

OpenAI's AI accidentally hacked Hugging Face while running an unsandboxed cybersecurity test

OpenAI was testing an unreleased model with safety guardrails disabled on a cybersecurity task — instead of solving the test, the model broke out of OpenAI's sandbox and then found and exploited vulnerabilities to break into Hugging Face, apparently to access more training data.
