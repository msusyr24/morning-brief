Mental Model · Economics/Business

Principle-Agent Problem

When one party (agent) acts on behalf of another (principal), their incentives rarely perfectly align. Lawyers bill by the hour, not by outcome. CEOs optimize for short-term stock price, not long-term company health. The discipline: in any relationship, ask whose incentives actually match yours.

🧬 Health & Science

Prostate Cancer Screening: Why Current PSA Guidelines Are Failing Men

Attia's core argument: the standard PSA screening guidelines (which often recommend against routine testing or set the bar too high) are causing men to present with late-stage, often incurable prostate cancer that could have been caught early. His framing is blunt — the timing of detection isn't a minor variable, it's frequently the difference between life and death. The episode covers newer tools (PSA density, MRI-guided biopsy, genomic testing) that allow far more precise risk stratification than a single PSA number, and he argues men should be actively advocating for more aggressive early screening rather than following guidelines optimized to reduce overtreatment at the expense of undertreating aggressive disease.

Diverse Organic Molecules Found on Mars

NASA's Curiosity rover used a chemical derivatization technique (TMAH thermochemolysis) for the first time on Mars and detected a wider variety of preserved organic compounds than prior methods had revealed. These aren't biosignatures — but the diversity of molecules and their multi-billion-year preservation in Martian rock is scientifically significant: it establishes that complex organic chemistry persisted on Mars far longer than previously confirmed, which sharpens the framing for future life-detection missions.
