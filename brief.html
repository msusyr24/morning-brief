Opportunity Cost

The true cost of any choice is the best alternative you gave up. Munger: 'Intelligent people make decisions based on opportunity costs.' If your money earns 8% elsewhere, a 6% opportunity is actually a loss of 2%. Applied broadly: every yes to one thing is a no to others.

GTO Wizard

GTO Wizard launched their PLO solver tool and ran a live stream walkthrough with Tombos21 and Joey Ingram. Key insights: PLO ranges call far more and three-bet far less pre-flop than No-Limit Hold'em, because equities run much closer and playing in position is more valuable than bloating pots out of position. In a HJ vs SB three-bet pot, 86% of four-bet ranges consist of hands containing aces — meaning KK is almost always a fold to a four-bet, a counterintuitive but mathematically sound adjustment for hold'em players. Tombos emphasizes that hand strength at the moment doesn't matter as much as nut potential by the river, and that back-door draws and future blockers are the real currency of PLO decisions.

Thomas DeLauer

DeLauer makes a useful mitochondrial adaptation framework: HIIT creates large acute spikes in PGC-1α (through calcium flux, AMPK activation, and reactive oxygen species) that drive mitochondrial biogenesis — more and stronger mitochondria. Zone 2 / low-intensity work doesn't multiply mitochondria as much but improves their efficiency and promotes mitochondrial fusion, allowing dysfunctional mitochondria to borrow resources from healthier ones. A key meta-analysis finding: head-to-head, HIIT and moderate-intensity continuous training produce equivalent improvements in maximum fat oxidation, just via different routes — HIIT by expanding respiratory capacity, steady-state by direct fat utilization practice. The practical prescription: HIIT adds horsepower; Zone 2 upgrades the cooling system. Doing only HIIT without the base is like adding horsepower before fixing the brakes.

🧬 Health & Science

Bimagrumab Phase 2: 100% Fat-Loss, Muscle Gain Without Exercise

A 2026 Nature Medicine paper on bimagrumab — a monoclonal antibody targeting the myostatin pathway — found that in obese participants, every gram of weight lost came from fat while participants simultaneously gained muscle mass and grip strength, with no change in physical activity. This is a pharmacological first: prior obesity drugs (including GLP-1s) lose 25-39% of weight as lean mass. The drug mimics a rare natural mutation (the "Hercules gene") found in double-muscled cattle and a handful of humans. It has completed Phase 2 trials; not yet approved. Side effects were mild (GI upset, headaches). Nick Norwitz's video is the best accessible breakdown.

Isometric Training: The Evidence Is Better Than You Think

Stronger by Science's comprehensive review finds no strong evidence that isometric training is inferior to dynamic training for strength or hypertrophy — particularly when performed at long muscle lengths. Given how underrepresented isometrics are in standard programming, this suggests a potentially underutilized tool for both rehabilitation contexts and general strength work.

Stronger by Science

HIIT vs. Zone 2: Different Mechanisms, Similar Fat Oxidation Outcomes

A meta-analysis in the Journal of Exercise Science and Fitness comparing HIIT and moderate-intensity continuous training in overweight adults found no meaningful difference in maximum fat oxidation improvement — but the mechanisms diverge: HIIT expands mitochondrial respiratory capacity, while steady-state training directly trains fat utilization and improves mitochondrial fusion dynamics. The practical implication from DeLauer's breakdown: both are necessary, and doing only HIIT without the aerobic base creates adaptation without the recovery infrastructure to support it.

Seed Oil Processing: The Hexane Concern Is Probably Not the Problem

In a detailed exchange with Layne Norton, Peter Attia works through the industrial chemistry: hexane residuals in finished seed oils are 0.05–0.5 parts per million, hexane toxicity is an inhalation rather than ingestion risk, and the math on lethal oral doses is absurd (~11,000kg). The more defensible concern is the shift in dietary linoleic acid from under 3% to ~10% of total calories over the past century — which isn't a processing story, it's a volume-of-consumption story. 💡 Worth Reading

What appear to be biochemical processes may be a natural feature of geology

Quanta Magazine piece exploring the possibility that some processes we've attributed to life — and used as biosignatures — may arise spontaneously from geological chemistry. If true, this has significant implications for the search for extraterrestrial life and for how we interpret ambiguous biological-looking signals in ancient Earth rocks and elsewhere. A genuinely unsettling finding for anyone who thinks the line between chemistry and biology is clean.
