Peter Attia (with Layne Norton)

A rigorous breakdown of the seed oil processing question. Norton's core claim: the actual hexane extraction used in seed oil production runs at low temperatures (~69°C) for short durations, and residual hexane in finished oils is 0.05–0.5 ppm — far below any meaningful toxicity threshold (you'd need to consume ~11,000 kg of oil at once for mild side effects, and hexane toxicity is primarily an inhalation risk anyway). The more interesting question they raise: linoleic acid content has risen from ~3% to ~10% of total food availability over the past century — and that shift, rather than trace industrial residues, may be the more important variable to examine. The honest conclusion is that the pure-form chemistry of seed oils looks relatively benign in isolation, but the real-world dietary context (massive increase in consumption, heating and reheating in cooking, displacement of other fats) remains an open question.

Prostate Cancer Screening: Why Current PSA Guidelines Are Failing Men

Peter Attia's argument: the timing of prostate cancer detection is "arguably the determining factor in life and death," but current PSA guidelines are too conservative and too infrequent to catch aggressive cancers early enough when cure rates are highest. The episode covers modern tools that go beyond standard PSA — including PSA density, PSA velocity, 4Kscore, and MRI-guided biopsy — that meaningfully improve the signal-to-noise ratio and reduce both overdiagnosis of indolent cancers and underdiagnosis of dangerous ones. Worth flagging for any man over 40: the mainstream guideline advice to start screening at 50 (or skip it) is directly challenged here with survival data.

Diverse Organic Molecules on Mars Revealed by the First SAM TMAH Experiment

NASA's Curiosity rover detected a broad range of organic compounds on Mars using a new wet chemistry technique (TMAH thermochemolysis) for the first time — including fatty acids, aromatic compounds, and other carbon-rich molecules preserved for potentially billions of years. This doesn't confirm life, but it does confirm that Mars has a richer organic chemistry than previously characterized, and that the preservation conditions are better than expected. The methodology matters: this technique can detect compounds that previous pyrolysis experiments would have destroyed.

The Vercel Breach: OAuth Attack Exposes Risk in Platform Environment Variables

A real security incident with broad implications: attackers exploited an OAuth flow to gain access to environment variables stored in Vercel — where many developers store API keys, database credentials, and secrets. The attack vector is OAuth misuse at the platform level, not individual app vulnerabilities, meaning developers who did everything "right" at the app layer were still exposed. Worth understanding the threat model if you use any platform that stores env vars.
