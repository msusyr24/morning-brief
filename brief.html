GTO Wizard (feat. Tombos21 & Joey Ingram)

GTO Wizard's first-ever live stream walked through the newly launched PLO solver tool, and the strategic insights are genuinely useful for any hold'em player curious about PLO. Key insight from Tombos: pre-flop ranges in PLO skew heavily toward calling rather than 3-betting because equities run much closer — Joey notes that on the button you almost never fold because four-card hands can connect on almost any board. The most counterintuitive finding demonstrated live: in a 4-bet pot, the 4-betting range is ~86% pocket aces, which means hands like KK become near-folds (the "second nuts" problem), while low connected hands may actually continue better. Tombos's broader thesis: the hold'em heuristic of "check to the pre-flop aggressor" breaks down in PLO because leading ranges exist far more frequently — the deeper principle is that hand strength NOW matters less than nut potential BY THE RIVER.

Jared Alderman

Alderman's guest Melanie makes a sharp distinction between poker as performance vs. practice: most players treat their study sessions as "practice" and their actual hands as "performances," but that framing is backwards — the hand IS the practice. Her core coaching thesis is that the gap between good and great players isn't technical knowledge but the delta between best-possible performance and actual output, which she estimates is enormous and largely invisible to the player. She transitioned from poker coaching into trading performance consulting after noticing that even talented traders had zero emotional process — one client informally estimated the work saved him ~$500K in six months. The key insight on tilt: good players muscle through it assuming their B-game is good enough; great players simply stop playing if they're not at A-game.

Peter Attia (feat. Layne Norton)

Attia and Norton dig into the industrial processing argument against seed oils — the most substantive version of the concern, setting aside pure linoleic acid debates. Norton walks through the hexane extraction process specifically: residual hexane in finished oils is 0.05–0.5 parts per million, hexane toxicity comes from inhalation not ingestion, and the LD50 math means you'd need to consume ~11,000 kg of oil to achieve even mild side effects. His more honest concession: industrial heating during processing can drive oxidation of the oil itself, though he argues meaningful oxidation requires >200°C for hours — longer than extraction processing typically runs. The unresolved question Attia pushes on is whether chronic low-level exposure over a lifetime could contribute to disease through mechanisms we haven't yet measured, which Norton acknowledges can't be fully ruled out.

🧬 Health & Science

Gene Therapy That Permanently Silences PCSK9

Peter Attia covers an early trial for a one-time gene therapy targeting PCSK9 — the protein that degrades LDL receptors. By permanently silencing PCSK9 expression, a single treatment could produce the equivalent of lifelong high-dose statin therapy without ongoing medication. This is mechanistically distinct from existing PCSK9 inhibitor drugs (injections every few weeks); it's a durable edit. Still early-stage, but Attia frames it as potentially the beginning of the end of atherosclerosis as a leading killer.

Isometric Training Is Not Inferior for Strength or Hypertrophy

The Stronger by Science review challenges the common dismissal of isometric training. The current evidence does not support the claim that isometrics produce meaningfully worse strength or size adaptations than dynamic training — and in some contexts (joint angles, injury rehab, connective tissue loading) isometrics may be preferable. Worth revisiting if you've written off isometric work as accessory filler.

TMS as Rapid-Onset Depression Treatment (Dr. Nolan Williams)

Williams's Stanford Neuromodulation Therapy achieves full remission in 1–5 days by stimulating the dorsolateral prefrontal cortex in a dense 5-day protocol — no drug, no serotonin manipulation. The mechanism: the DLPFC directly governs the cingulate and ultimately the heart via vagus nerve; depression is a failure of the DLPFC to "govern" the cingulate's conflict detection loop. This reframes depression as a recalibratable circuit rather than a chemical deficiency — with the implication that patients who remit feel genuinely recovered, not chemically managed. Some patients report spontaneous present-moment awareness ("mindfulness") by Thursday of the treatment week, unprompted.

Scientists have successfully synthesized boron buckyballs (B80), disproving longstanding theoretical predictions that these cage structures were chemically impossible. This is the kind of "we were confidently wrong" result in materials science that tends to open new research directions — boron nanostructures have potential applications in semiconductors and drug delivery.

Is Work From Home Bad for Your Mental Health?

A study finding that workers in remotable jobs spent approximately one additional hour alone per workday after the pandemic, with differentially more days spent entirely alone and less after-work socializing — compared to non-remotable job workers. The mental health implications are the obvious concern, but the documented mechanism (increased solitude, reduced incidental social contact) is the useful finding for thinking about how you structure your own work week.

ChatGPT "Dreaming": New Memory Architecture

OpenAI has shipped a new memory system for ChatGPT called "Dreaming" — instead of only storing explicit facts you tell it, the system now synthesizes and consolidates memory from past conversations during low-usage periods, similar to how sleep consolidates human memory. The practical result is that ChatGPT should now maintain more coherent context about your preferences and working style across sessions without you having to manually manage memory entries — a meaningful quality-of-life shift for daily users.
