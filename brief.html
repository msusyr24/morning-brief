GTO Lab

A sharp, brief observation on victimhood as one of poker's most common and insidious mental traps. The point: it's easy to convince yourself you're being run bad or that opponents are just getting lucky, and that narrative feels liberating in the moment but blocks honest self-assessment. The antidote offered is deliberate solo processing — sitting with the data, being honest about whether an opponent has actually improved, and then deciding whether to compete harder or redirect energy entirely. The framing is unusually mature: acknowledging someone is better than you in certain spots isn't defeat, it's information.

  • Dr. Sten Ekberg: Detailed breakdown of why belly fat accumulates differently after 50 — testosterone decline drives aromatase upregulation (converting remaining T to estrogen) in men, while estrogen loss at menopause redirects fat from hips/thighs to visceral storage in women. Both pathways worsen insulin resistance in a self-reinforcing cycle; two-thirds of the effect is lifestyle-addressable. Recommends cutting refined carbs/sugar, time-restricted eating (14–16hr overnight fast), and resistance training for glycogen storage capacity — not steady-state cardio, which raises cortisol and deposits visceral fat. I don't think AI will make your processes go faster

A contrarian take (557 points on HN) arguing that AI doesn't accelerate broken or bureaucratic processes — it just makes the broken parts more visible, or in some cases amplifies dysfunction by generating output faster than organizations can absorb and act on it. The argument is structural rather than technical: speed gains from AI are captured only when the surrounding process can actually move faster, and most organizations can't. Worth sitting with if you're thinking about where AI actually creates leverage versus where it creates the illusion of it.

Crystals Found Inside Wreckage from the First Nuclear Bomb Test

Scientists identified unusual crystalline structures inside the trinitite (glassy material) formed at the Trinity nuclear test site in 1945.
