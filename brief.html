🧬 Health & Science

Brain Lipidology: APOE, Cholesterol Homeostasis, and Alzheimer's Risk

Tom Dayspring's core claim: the brain's cholesterol system is almost entirely separate from the peripheral lipoprotein system — plasma LDL levels have essentially no bearing on brain cholesterol supply, because the blood-brain barrier blocks lipoprotein exchange. The brain holds 20–25g of cholesterol (vs. ~3–5g in the liver) and synthesizes its own locally. The popular fear that aggressively lowering LDL will "deprive the brain" is, in Dayspring's words, simply not true. The episode also covers APOE genotype's outsized influence on Alzheimer's risk and how lipid-lowering therapies interact (or don't) with brain cholesterol homeostasis.

Peter Attia – Episode 395

What Does the Evidence Actually Say About Isometric Training for Strength and Hypertrophy?

Stronger by Science deep-dive challenging the conventional wisdom that isometric (static hold) training is inferior to dynamic training for strength and size. The evidence reviewed suggests isometrics can produce comparable strength gains, especially at the joint angle trained, and aren't the dead-end many programs treat them as. The article distinguishes between yielding isometrics (holding against gravity) and overcoming isometrics (pushing against an immovable object), which have meaningfully different stimulus profiles.

Stronger by Science

Sleep Restriction Increases Caloric Intake ~300 Cal/Day — Via Different Mechanisms in Men vs. Women

From the Huberman/St-Onge episode: a controlled crossover study (9-hr vs. 4-hr sleep opportunity for 5 nights) found subjects ate ~300 extra calories/day when sleep-restricted. The sex-specific split is the novel finding: men showed elevated ghrelin (the hunger-driving hormone); women showed reduced GLP-1 (the satiety-suppressing hormone). Earlier studies had only been run in men, which is why the ghrelin finding had been replicated but the GLP-1 pathway was missed. Brain imaging showed upregulated reward centers for food stimuli under sleep restriction, adding a behavioral layer on top of the hormonal one.

German Court: Google Liable for False Answers in AI Overviews

A German court ruled that AI Overview summaries constitute Google's own speech — not a neutral aggregation of third-party content — making Google directly liable for factual errors they contain. This is the first ruling of its kind and sets a potential precedent for how AI-generated summaries from search engines are treated legally across Europe. Has real implications for how aggressively Google (and others) can deploy answer-style AI features without editorial review.
