🎥 From People I Follow

Worth your time:

GTO Lab

The WSOP mindset video with coach Nikki P is genuinely good process-over-results content. The core argument: your stack is not your scoreboard — even when you're below starting stack in a $10k event, you're still playing high stakes and every decision still matters at full weight. The practical framework is about eliminating small decisions before you arrive: pre-plan lodging, transportation, meals, and bathroom strategy so your cognitive load at the table is reserved for poker. Nikki P's specific tactic on dinner break is worth noting — he deliberately skips socializing entirely, takes a shower, watches 30 minutes of a show, and resets mentally, arguing that the cumulative drain of 30 days of dinner-table hand history conversations is a real performance tax. The decision-minimization framing (pre-bought snacks, pre-made reservation, knowing your route out before break ends) is a direct application of ego depletion logic to tournament poker.

GTO Wizard

The PLO launch stream with Tombos21 and Joey Ingram surfaces a genuinely interesting theory point for hold'em players crossing over: in PLO, your current hand strength is largely irrelevant — what matters is your hand's nut potential by the river. This reframes the entire way you evaluate starting hands. Practically: when facing a 4-bet in PLO, GTO Wizard data shows ~86% of that range contains aces, which means KK is often a clear fold (it will flop the second nuts too often and has poor nut potential against an aces-heavy range). The pre-flop calling vs. 3-betting distinction is also notable — PLO GTO calls far more and 3-bets far less than NLH, because equities run close, pot inflation out of position is expensive, and 4-card hands play well multi-way. Tombos's insight that studying the same concept in two different games reveals the deeper underlying principle (rather than a game-specific heuristic) is a useful meta-point about how to study poker.

₿ Bitcoin & Macro

Full Text of Strategic Bitcoin Reserve Bill Officially Published

H.R. 8957's full text reveals the proposed U.S. Strategic Bitcoin Reserve would impose a 20-year lock-up on all federally held bitcoin, with mandatory proof-of-reserve attestations. This is the most consequential structural detail yet — it signals that legislators are designing this as a sovereign savings vehicle, not a trading position, which directionally tracks the Saylor thesis of bitcoin as a long-duration store-of-value asset for nation-states.

🧬 Health & Science

Brain Lipidology: APOE, Cholesterol, Alzheimer's Risk, and Lipid-Lowering Therapies — Tom Dayspring

Tom Dayspring directly challenges the widespread belief that lowering LDL too aggressively will deprive or damage the brain — calling it flatly untrue. The brain synthesizes its own cholesterol locally (it doesn't rely on circulating LDL crossing the blood-brain barrier), meaning systemic lipid-lowering therapies don't reduce brain cholesterol supply. The episode also covers how APOE genotype modulates Alzheimer's risk through lipid transport mechanisms in the CNS — a mechanistic link between cardiovascular lipidology and neurodegeneration that's clinically underappreciated.

Peter Attia — Episode #395

The Beginning of the End of Atherosclerosis?

Attia covers an early-stage gene therapy trial targeting PCSK9 — the protein that regulates LDL receptor recycling. The approach would permanently silence PCSK9 expression with a single treatment, producing profound and sustained LDL reduction without daily statins or injectable PCSK9 inhibitors. Still early-trial data, but the mechanism is clean and the implications for cardiovascular disease prevention are significant if it holds.

Hold Still: What Does the Evidence Actually Say About Isometric Training?

Stronger by Science reviews the current literature on isometric (static contraction) training and finds the conventional wisdom — that it's substantially inferior to dynamic training for strength and hypertrophy — isn't well-supported. The piece distinguishes between angle-specific vs. joint-angle-generalized strength gains and examines where isometrics may actually be advantageous, particularly for tendon health and rehab contexts. Useful for anyone programming around injury or looking to add variation with evidence behind it.
