GTO Wizard

GTO Wizard benchmarked major LLMs against their poker AI in heads-up play. GPT 5.3 performed "best" but still lost at 16.3 bb/100 — catastrophically bad compared to human experts who lose around 5 bb/100. Key findings: LLMs have terrible preflop ranges (folding way too much), frequently misread their own hands (calling Q9o "suited," misreading board textures), and hero-call far too often with weak holdings. The testing used AVAT methodology for luck-adjusted win rates. GTO Wizard opened a public API for anyone to benchmark their models. The failures reveal LLMs are predicting language patterns from poker content, not actually computing EV — they sound strategic but make objectively poor decisions.

Nick Norwitz

Norwitz argues the "lower LDL is better" paradigm misses something important: genetic variants that lower LDL (like PCSK9 loss-of-function) show no lifespan benefit — if anything, trending toward earlier death. But ApoC3 loss-of-function variants are 2.5x more common in centenarians, despite similar LDL/ApoB levels. The difference: ApoC3 variants improve triglyceride clearance and metabolic efficiency (43% drop in remnant cholesterol, only 4% drop in LDL). ApoC3 slows triglyceride breakdown; lowering it improves lipid metabolism and insulin sensitivity. Evidence-based ways to lower ApoC3: ketogenic diets (one study showed 50% reduction in 2 weeks), fructose/sugar restriction, omega-3s (target 8%+ omega-3 index), and improving insulin sensitivity generally.

Thomas DeLauer

Caffeine timing study: A Journal of International Society of Sports Nutrition study found 3mg/kg caffeine at 8am increased max fat oxidation by 10.7%, but at 5pm it increased by 29%. Theory: the delta from baseline cortisol is larger in the evening. Practical application: if you can tolerate it without disrupting sleep (6 hours minimum before bed), midday caffeine before lower-intensity cardio may boost fat burning. Recommends combining with apigenin to reduce stimulatory effects while preserving fat oxidation benefits.

Melatonin deep-dive with neuroscientist: After 30 years of daily melatonin use, guest measured his endogenous production after stopping — completely normal levels, no dependency. No evidence of suppressing LH, FSH, or testosterone. Primary benefits aren't sleep (just reduces sleep latency) but neuroprotection: crosses blood-brain barrier, potent antioxidant that suppresses superoxide anion from mitochondria. 5mg sublingual is his dose. Caution: cancer patients on chemo/radiation should be careful since those kill cancer cells via oxidative stress. Green tea extract supplements can cause liver damage at high doses despite benefits at normal tea consumption levels.

Renaissance Periodization

Dr. Mike's beginner-to-advanced training framework: Beginners (0-2 years) should train 2-4x/week whole body with distributed compounds, sets of 5-10 reps, focusing on technique over intensity. They grow from minimal volume — don't rush to intermediate programming. Intermediates (3-6 years) need more variation and volume. Advanced (7+ years) make slow gains and shouldn't claim that status if technique is poor. Key principle: "If you can maintain beginner results with beginner volumes, don't go do intermediate shit." Milk each stage. On sleep: 70% of daily growth hormone releases in the first half of sleep; poor sleep raises cortisol (catabolic, promotes visceral fat storage) and directly reduces muscle protein synthesis that occurs during sleep hours.

Human Accelerated Region 1

HAR1 is the fastest-evolving region in the human genome compared to chimps — 18 changes where only 2 would be expected. It's active during cortical development weeks 7-19 of gestation and may be key to what makes human brains distinctly human. Fascinating intersection of genetics and what separates us from other primates.
