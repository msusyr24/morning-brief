₿ Bitcoin & Macro

SpaceX Files for IPO With $1.45 Billion Bitcoin Treasury on Its Books

SpaceX has filed for a Nasdaq IPO revealing it holds 18,712 BTC (~$1.45B), placing it among the largest corporate Bitcoin holders. This matters as a data point in the Saylor-thesis trajectory: major industrial companies (not just financial firms) now carrying Bitcoin on the balance sheet as they enter public markets, normalizing the treasury strategy further.

U.S. Lawmaker Introduces Bill to Codify Strategic Bitcoin Reserve

Rep. Nick Begich (R-AK) introduced legislation to permanently establish a U.S. strategic Bitcoin reserve, drawing bipartisan support. This moves the reserve concept from executive order (easily reversible) toward legislative permanence — a meaningful institutional escalation if it gains traction.

🧬 Health & Science

Hold Still: What Does the Evidence Actually Say About Isometric Training for Strength and Hypertrophy?

Stronger by Science digs into whether isometric training (holding a contraction without joint movement) is actually inferior for strength and hypertrophy — the common assumption. The piece reviews the current evidence and argues that assumption isn't well-supported: isometrics can drive meaningful strength and size adaptations, particularly when matched for effort and volume. Specific mechanisms and study findings are covered, making this relevant for programming decisions around injury, joint stress, or training variety.

An OpenAI Model Has Disproved a Central Conjecture in Discrete Geometry

An OpenAI model solved the 80-year-old "unit distance problem," one of Erdős's favorite open questions in discrete geometry — not just found a proof, but produced a disproof of a widely-held conjecture. Mathematician Tim Gowers called it a genuine milestone. This is the kind of AI mathematical reasoning result that changes how seriously the field takes AI as a research collaborator, not just a tool.

Someone ran a 31-billion-parameter local AI model (using 50GB of swap space) on a consumer MacBook to index and make searchable a full year of personal video footage — entirely offline, no cloud. How General Semantics Was Used to Treat World War II Veterans

Donald Robertson (author of How to Think Like a Roman Emperor) writes about Douglas Kelley, the psychiatrist who treated Nuremberg defendants, and how he applied General Semantics — a precursor to cognitive-behavioral approaches — as a rational psychotherapy for combat trauma. Interesting both as intellectual history and as an early documented example of structured language/framing techniques being used to reshape psychological response to adversity.

Fertility and Financial Risk-Taking

Using nationally representative data from three countries, researchers found that childless adults who don't expect children are 21–36% more likely to invest in stocks than those who expect children, controlling for personal characteristics. An underappreciated driver of individual financial behavior and risk tolerance — has implications for how life stage shapes decision-making frameworks.
