Mental Model · Epistemics

Survivorship Bias

We see the winners and forget the losers who didn't make it. Successful entrepreneurs' advice is biased toward the strategies of survivors, ignoring the identical strategies of people who failed. WWII example: engineers wanted to armor the parts of returning planes with bullet holes — until Abraham Wald pointed out the planes that didn't return were hit elsewhere.

Worth your time:

GTO Wizard

Tom Boss 21 walks Joey Ingram through a unified framework for river poker math, arguing that pot odds, MDF, alpha, and bluffing frequency are all expressions of the same underlying relationship — not four separate concepts. The core insight: on the river, the pot odds you lay your opponent should exactly equal your bluffing frequency, and vice versa. Using a GTO Wizard node-lock simulation, he demonstrates that shifting big blind's bluffing range by just a few combos flips all bluff-catchers from indifference to pure fold or pure call — the river is that sensitive. Blocker effects are briefly covered: hands blocking the opponent's likely bluffing range (e.g., holding a 6 on a 5-4-4-3-3 board) make better calls, but in practice human ranges are too wide for this to matter much.

Nick Norwitz

Heart rate variability (HRV) is framed as a real-time window into autonomic nervous system balance — specifically parasympathetic (vagal) tone. Key data points: in an 808-patient post-MI cohort study (American Journal of Cardiology, 1987), low HRV predicted a 5.3-fold higher mortality risk over years; in a 2,501-person prospective study, each one-standard-deviation drop in HRV was associated with a 47% increased risk of a first cardiac event. Practical improvement levers: zone 2 cardio is the most reliable baseline builder; HIIT can drive further gains but requires dosing (too much suppresses HRV via poor recovery — he suggests ~70/30 zone 2 to HIIT). Omega-3s (EPA+DHA, 3,000–4,000 mg/day) are supported by multiple RCTs, operating via membrane fluidity, inflammation reduction, and brain-stem vagal tone. Caffeine acutely suppresses HRV but is neutral-to-beneficial at up to 4 cups/day overall. Measure consistently (morning resting or overnight wearable — pick one and stick with it).

Stronger by Science: Hypertrophy–Strength Correlation Revisited

A new analysis suggests that muscle growth contributes more to strength gains in novice lifters than prior studies indicated — but the relationship is still far from linear, and hypertrophy explains only a portion of strength variance. The takeaway: in beginners, don't treat size and strength as separate goals; in advanced trainees, the divergence remains real and programming should reflect it.
