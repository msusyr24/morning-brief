GTO Wizard

A strong PLO study framework from a player who climbed from PL100 to high stakes: the core mistake is treating solver study like data collection (writing down every frequency) instead of pattern extraction. The "SIP method" — Simplify, Identify 80/20 spots, find repeating Principles — argues you should build heuristics, not memorize combos. Concrete examples: in PLO, opening ranges across positions share a core structure (cutoff anchors most), so you learn one and adjust at the margins rather than studying each position from scratch. On sizing: divide all flops into "locked" (paired/monotone/straight-heavy) where nuts don't change much → use 1/4 to 1/3, and "regular" flops → default half pot. The key post-flop skill is finding *threshold groups* — the hand strength at which strategy flips from pure fold to pure call/raise — and ignoring everything else. This is one of the cleaner "how to actually use a solver" frameworks I've seen.

Videos: How Pros Actually Study PLO

GTO Lab

Nick Petrangelo makes a nuanced ICM/pre-flop argument: KQs from the small blind at ~30bb is better as a flat call than a jam, despite being a premium hand with good blocker properties. His reasoning — KQs keeps dominated hands in (KJ, KT, QJ) that would fold to a jam, plays well post-flop, and the ICM pressure on the raiser is already doing work. The deeper principle: from the small blind, you want *clearance* (jams), but KQs is too good to give up post-flop value for that clearance. Contrast with big blind where you're closing action and should defend more polarly.

Videos: King-queen suited looks like a clean 30bb jam - Nick Petrangelo in Tournament Savagery

Jared Alderman

Kevin Rabichow makes a subtle point about "betting for information" — his claim is that if you're deviating from GTO to gather information, it should manifest as *checking* more (to see what your opponent does), not betting thinner. Betting thinner to "find out what they call you with" is usually just a rationalization for marginal bluffs. The example: middle pair no draw on a paired board, where checking is default but a bet might exploit a specific player type — Rabichow's pushback is that the "information" you get when they fold tells you almost nothing useful.

Nick Norwitz

Covers Russian peptide research (Khavinson's work) — a 2003 double-blind 6-year trial using thymus and pineal gland extracts reported fourfold reduction in mortality in adults over 60, alongside improvements in blood pressure, physical function, and heart disease. The refined peptides from this work include epitalon (activates telomerase, associated with *lower* cancer risk despite the theoretical concern — possibly because thymic peptides simultaneously improve immune surveillance), thymic peptides broadly, and pinealon (orally bioavailable, acts on circadian rhythm and sleep architecture via pineal gland pathways). Norwitz tried pinealon himself and reports dramatically increased REM sleep, including vivid layered dreams. The mechanistic resolution to the telomerase-cancer paradox: epitalon elongates telomeres while thymic peptides regenerate the adaptive immune system's cancer surveillance — regeneration + surveillance together, not regeneration alone. Worth flagging: all evidence here is from small trials, some Russian, some replication uncertain — Norwitz is clear about this even while endorsing personal use.

🧬 Health & Science

Nick Norwitz: Russian Longevity Peptides — Epitalon, Thymic Peptides, Pinealon

The most substantive health science item this cycle. Khavinson's 2003 double-blind 6-year human trial (adults 60+) using thymus and pineal gland extracts reported fourfold mortality reduction vs. controls — a huge effect size for a small trial. The mechanism refinement over subsequent decades isolated specific peptides: epitalon activates telomerase (telomere elongation), which theoretically risks cancer but paradoxically correlates with *lower* cancer incidence in trials; thymic peptides regenerate adaptive immune surveillance, plausibly explaining the cancer paradox by pairing cellular renewal with immune policing; pinealon is orally bioavailable (unusually for peptides), targets pineal gland circadian/sleep pathways, and has anecdotal reports from physicians of doubling REM sleep. Norwitz tried pinealon himself and reports the most restorative sleep in over a year. Caveats: trials are small, some Russian-origin with replication uncertainty, and the oral bioavailability mechanism for large molecules remains not fully understood (endocytosis is the leading hypothesis). Worth monitoring as the peptide category grows. See the full video for primary data links.

Visceral Fat Mechanisms: Trans Fats, Fructose, and Artificial Sweeteners

Thomas DeLauer's video is unusually mechanism-dense for the format. Key data points: (1) A 6-year primate study in Obesity showed trans fat groups gained significantly more visceral fat than monounsaturated fat groups at identical maintenance calories — meaning the fat type, not the caloric surplus, drove visceral accumulation, likely via disruption of essential fatty acid desaturation and downstream insulin resistance. (2) A 559-subject study in Journal of Nutrition linked high HFCS intake to visceral fat, elevated CRP, and insulin resistance — the same feedback loop. (3) A ~1,500-person 10-year study found chronic artificial sweetener users had 2.6cm larger waist circumference and 53% higher abdominal obesity — proposed mechanism: gut dysbiosis → impaired glucose tolerance → insulin resistance, with a Nature landmark study showing this dysbiosis is real and replicable. The unified thread: all three drive visceral fat via insulin resistance, independent of caloric intake in some cases.

Stronger by Science: Hypertrophy-Strength Correlation Revisited

New analysis suggests muscle growth contributes more to strength gains in novice lifters than prior studies implied — the correlation is stronger than previously measured. However, the piece is careful to note this relationship is far from deterministic and weakens as training age increases, meaning more experienced lifters have a lower portion of their strength gains explained by hypertrophy. Practical implication: early in a training career, prioritizing hypertrophy volume may have a double payoff (size + strength), but that leverage decreases over time.

Peter Attia: Endometriosis & Adenomyosis Deep Dive

Key facts from the interview worth knowing: endometriosis affects ~10% of reproductive-age women (200M globally), but 30–50% of infertile women — causally, not just correlatively. Adenomyosis (endometrial tissue invading the myometrium) is even more prevalent at 20–30%, with 70% overlap with endometriosis patients. The modern reproductive pattern (four times more ovulatory cycles than 200 years ago due to later menarche, later first pregnancy, fewer children, less breastfeeding) is likely a primary driver of rising endometriosis prevalence — the body didn't evolve for this many retrograde menstruation events. Both diseases share estrogen dominance, progesterone resistance, and somatic oncogenic mutations in lesions, yet behave as benign tumors — high-powered growth machinery constrained by surrounding fibrosis. Mechanistically dense and well worth the full episode if you care about women's health or want to understand how lifestyle factors interact with reproductive physiology.

The mental model is broadly useful: when you see a step-change in any metric (health outcomes, financial returns, model benchmarks), the first question should be whether the discontinuity reflects reality or a measurement regime change. A 2020 piece that remains relevant given how much benchmark gaming now shapes AI capability claims.
