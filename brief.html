GTO Wizard

GTO Wizard launched its first-ever PLO tool and streamed a live walkthrough with Tombos21 and Joey Ingram. The key strategic insight surfaced early: in PLO, 86% of four-bet ranges consist of AA-xx, making hands like KK a pure fold in four-bet pots — a complete inversion of NLHE intuition. Tombos makes the deeper point that PLO forces you to abandon "current hand strength" as a frame and instead evaluate "what will my hand be by the river?" — making backdoor draws, future blockers, and reverse blockers far more central than in hold'em. The calling-vs-3betting frequency difference between games is also highlighted: PLO players call far more and three-bet far less, because equities run closer preflop and inflating pots OOP is more costly when four cards mean every flop can connect.

Peter Attia (feat. Layne Norton)

A detailed breakdown of seed oil processing chemistry, specifically whether the industrial extraction process — not just linoleic acid itself — drives harm. Norton's argument: hexane used in extraction evaporates at 69°C, leaves residues of 0.05–0.5 parts per million in finished oils, doesn't bioaccumulate, and toxicity cases come only from inhalation, not ingestion. His calculation: you'd need to consume ~11,340kg of oil at once to even approach mild side effects from hexane. The more substantive concern he acknowledges is the shift in dietary linoleic acid from ~3% of food availability 100 years ago to roughly ~10% today — the cumulative displacement of the omega-6/omega-3 ratio being the more defensible mechanistic concern than the processing chemistry per se.

Dr. Sten Ekberg

Ekberg ate nothing but sardines for 5 days (~6 tins/day, ~1,300–1,400 kcal/day, ~120g protein, ~90g fat, near-zero carbs) and reported honest results: adequate but not thriving, with fatigue building by day 3. He attributes this partly to not being fully fat-adapted beforehand — he came off 70–120g carbs/day directly into zero carbs, rather than transitioning via a multi-week low-carb phase first. The nutritional case he makes is strong: sardines provide ~5,000mg EPA+DHA daily (roughly 50x the average American's intake), with a near 1:1 EPA:DHA ratio making them unusually brain-favorable. The calcium source (calcium hydroxyapatite from bones) is highlighted as far superior to calcium carbonate supplements — the former absorbs at any pH, the latter requires stomach acid to absorb, while simultaneously neutralizing that acid, making most calcium supplements essentially useless.

🧬 Health & Science

Bimagrumab Phase 2: 100% Fat Loss, Simultaneous Muscle Gain

A paper published in Nature Medicine (2026) on bimagrumab — a monoclonal antibody partially blocking the myostatin/activin receptor pathway — showed that in obese participants, every gram of weight lost came from fat while participants simultaneously gained lean mass and grip strength with no change in physical activity. This is mechanistically distinct from GLP-1 agonists, which cause 25–39% of weight loss as lean mass. The drug mimics the biology of myostatin-null individuals (double-muscled cattle, rare humans) who exhibit extraordinary musculature and low body fat with potential longevity advantages. Side effects were mild GI upset and headaches; the drug has completed Phase 2 and is not yet commercially available.

Seed Oil Processing: Hexane Residues Are Not the Mechanism of Harm

In the Attia/Norton conversation, Norton makes a specific quantitative case: hexane residues in seed oils run 0.05–0.5 ppm, hexane toxicity comes from inhalation not ingestion, and the estimated oral dose for mild side effects is ~11,340kg of oil — effectively ruling out hexane as a chronic harm driver. The more defensible concern he points to is the dietary linoleic acid share rising from ~3% to ~10% of food availability over the past century, which drives the omega-6/omega-3 imbalance. If you've been avoiding seed oils primarily due to processing concerns rather than the fatty acid ratio argument, this is worth updating on.

Berkeley CS professors are documenting a concrete and accelerating pattern: students using AI tools for coursework are showing degraded foundational math skills and higher failure rates — not just in introductory courses but in upper-division work that requires the skills they skipped building.
